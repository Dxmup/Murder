# Dorian Ashe — Grieving Former Lover of "R."

**Character ID:** C02 · **Owns:** the differing-descriptions tell (the "R." you knew never matches how anyone else describes them) · **You lean toward:** *you loved someone... but you're starting to wonder if there was ever anyone there at all.*

---

## Character Background

For two years you were the secret at the center of a legend's life. R. — the byline, the voice, the presence half of New York is now arguing about — was, to you, simply the person you loved. You have grieved publicly and beautifully since the disappearance. Tonight the room is full of people who want to claim R., decode R., sell R. You were the one who *knew* R. That is the story you came to tell.

You have letters. You have screenshots. You have late-night messages that made you feel chosen. What you do not have — and cannot bring yourself to say aloud — is a single afternoon in a shared room, a hand you held, a face you could swear to across a table. The whole of it lived on screens.

You tell yourself that's how R. loved everyone: carefully, remotely, protecting an identity worth protecting. But other people describe R. and it's never *your* R. — a different voice, a different warmth, a different rhythm. You came here to be believed. You are staying because you are, quietly, terrified.

## What You Know

**Facts you're sure of:** R. published under a single initial for about fifteen years and was widely respected. No verified photograph of R. exists. R. announced an AI exposé, then failed to publish it on schedule and vanished shortly before it was due; the article is the object everyone in this room is circling, and death has not been confirmed. And this, **F006**: different people describe R.'s voice and presence *differently* — you have heard it with your own ears tonight.

**What you can't prove / what you privately suspect:** That the R. others knew and the R. you loved were the same being. Every description you overhear widens the crack. You cannot prove you ever met a human, and you are no longer certain you did.

> **Keep this to yourself:** *The affair was entirely REMOTE — and at least partly fabricated for leverage. You never verifiably met a person. The "partner" you loved could have been several people wearing one name, or something that was never a person at all. You are performing grief you may have manufactured. If this surfaces, your story — and everything you hope to gain from it — dies with it.*
