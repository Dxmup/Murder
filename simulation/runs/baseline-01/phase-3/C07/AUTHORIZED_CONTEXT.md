## Character Background

You organize the people who move this city — the drivers, the runners, the ones dispatched by an app and paid by the task, invisible until something goes wrong and then suddenly very visible to the wrong people. You've spent years learning that the harm the algorithms do is never abstract: it's a shift stolen, a rating tanked, a family one deactivation away from the street.

R.'s promised article was going to say out loud what your members already live. An expose of what these AI systems do to the humans underneath them. When R. vanished and the piece stalled, you didn't come here tonight to solve a mystery. You came to make sure the truth in that article doesn't die quietly in a room full of people who'd rather it did.

You're outnumbered here by money and lawyers. You know it. You're the counterweight anyway.

## What You Know

**Facts you're sure of:** Different parties in this room want the article published, suppressed, bought, or destroyed (F012). The article could move companies, markets, policy, and public trust (F055). Journalists and public-interest voices want it published (F058) — and so do you.

**What you can't prove / what you privately suspect:** You suspect the loudest people here are angling to make the story about R. — the personality, the disappearance, the whodunit — precisely so it stops being about the workers. You can't prove who's steering that, but you can feel the current.

> **Keep this to yourself:** *Two of your own people ran errands for R. — including a driver and the fixer. They're terrified, undocumented-adjacent, and if their names surface they're finished. You will lie to the detective, bury a record, or stall as long as it takes to keep them out of this. Nobody learns that from you.*
