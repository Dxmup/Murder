# Nikos Alexopoulos — Restaurant Owner

**Character ID:** C14 · **Owns:** the delivery origin — the standing order and the restaurant's records of it · **You lean toward:** *somebody ordered that food, the same order, for years — of course there was a real person at the other end of it, even if you never once saw their face.*

---

## Character Background

You run a good restaurant. Not fashionable — good. You have run it long enough that certain tickets print themselves: same dish, same time, same address, for years. You never asked who ate it. In this business you learn that the customers who pay on time and never complain are the ones you protect by not asking.

Now that quiet little order has a name attached to it in the newspapers, and reporters are circling your dining room like they smell blood. You came tonight because if this is going to be spoken about, you would rather be in the room than be the empty chair everyone points at.

You are not a detective. You are a man with a kitchen, a payroll, and a lot to lose.

## What You Know

**Facts you're sure of:** For years, a regular order came from your restaurant to the same building (F020, F021). It never listed an apartment number (F022). The standing instruction was always the same: *leave the food in the lobby* (F023). Your restaurant's own records establish that pattern beyond dispute (F031), and that pattern is now something a lot of people badly want to talk about (F035).

**What you can't prove / what you privately suspect:** You took the orders; you never saw the diner. You assume one steady customer — but you're assuming. Somebody paid, somebody ate, and you filled in a face you never actually met.

> **Keep this to yourself:** A police, health, or immigration probe would crack open more than a standing order — you pay some staff off the books, including a cook who has no papers. You will deny or downplay the whole delivery pattern before you let inspectors walk your line. Protecting them is protecting yourself.

## Main Goal

Keep your restaurant's name out of a criminal scandal — and out of the health and immigration scrutiny that a police case drags in behind it.

## Sub-Goal One

Decide whether to sell your "regular customer" story to **Rick Salerno** for cash — or deny you ever noticed a thing.

## Sub-Goal Two

Recover the money still owed on the standing order — or spend that leverage instead protecting your undocumented cook from exposure.
