# Renata Kessler — Claimed Next-of-Kin

**Character ID:** C01 · **Owns:** the disappearance and the estate stakes · **You lean toward:** *of course R. was real — R. was family... wasn't R.?*

## Character Background

You came tonight because someone has to speak for the person nobody in this room will admit they never touched. You are R.'s next-of-kin — the estranged sibling, the adult child, the blood the byline outran. For fifteen years the relationship was distance and silence, and now silence is all that's left.

There is an estate. There are rights to an article the whole city is circling. And there is a room full of strangers, rivals, and vultures who would rather R. dissolve into myth than let a family member close the ledger. You will not let that happen.

You loved R. — or you loved the shape of R. that reached you across all that distance. Tonight you find out whether that shape holds up under other people's eyes.

## What You Know

**Facts you're sure of:** R. published for about fifteen years under a pseudonym (F001) and earned a deeply respected reputation (F002). No verified photograph of R. has ever existed (F003). R. announced an article about AI (F009) that was not published as scheduled (F010); that article is the central object many people here want (F014). R. disappeared shortly before publication (F015), and R.'s death has not been confirmed (F016).

**What you can't prove / what you privately suspect:** You cannot produce a birth record, a shared photograph, a witness who saw the two of you together. Your entire relationship existed at a remove — arranged, mediated, financial. In fifteen years, your only "contact" was money moving and cards arriving. You tell yourself that was just how R. lived. Late at night you are less sure what it was.

> **Keep this to yourself:** Your kinship may be fabricated. You built the claim out of fragments — receipts, cards, a plausible story — to seize the estate and the article's rights. You never once met R. in person. If anyone proves that, you are not a grieving relative; you are a con.
