## Character Background

You've held that door for two decades. You know which buzzers stick, which tenants tip at Christmas, which cabs idle too long. You are the institutional memory of the building — and tonight that memory is a liability.

For years a standing food order arrived every night, no apartment number, "leave it in the lobby." You never questioned it. You never had to; the lobby was your kingdom and things in it had a way of quietly disappearing before morning. Now a legendary journalist is gone, that lobby is the last known address on Earth, and everyone with a badge or a byline wants to know what you saw.

The honest answer terrifies you: almost nothing. And "almost nothing" from the man paid to see everything sounds a lot like negligence.

## What You Know

**Facts you're sure of:** A pseudonymous journalist published for roughly fifteen years (F001) with a highly respected reputation (F002), and no verified photograph of them exists (F003). You also know the ground truth of your own door: regular orders came from the same restaurant for years (F020), always to your building (F021), never with an apartment number (F022), with standing instructions to leave the food in the lobby (F023). Your shift ended shortly after each delivery (F026), and the building is undeniably tied to that delivery routine (F036).

**What you can't prove / what you privately suspect:** That the "resident" may be a fiction. Foot traffic through your lobby was heavy enough that being buzzed in never meant living there. The only human you ever actually watched handle those bags was the errand-runner — not a tenant, not a face you could name as R.

> **Keep this to yourself:** *There is a hole in the building footage that lands on you. For years you took tips to look the other way — packages and people through your door, unlogged, no questions. If that comes out, your job and maybe your pension go with it.*
