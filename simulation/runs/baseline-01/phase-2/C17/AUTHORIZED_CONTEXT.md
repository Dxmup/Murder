## Character Background

For six years you have run **The Questionry**, the online source-community where thousands of readers annotate R.'s work, trade theories, and grieve. You did not know R. You did not need to. You knew the *voice* — that unmistakable style — and the voice knew you back, or felt like it did. That was enough to build a life around.

When R. announced the AI exposé and then vanished, your inbox became a chapel and a war room at once. People come to you now to be told it meant something. You intend to keep telling them so.

You are in this room because the legend is being litigated tonight, and you refuse to let it be litigated by cynics.

## What You Know

- **Facts you're sure of:** R. published under a pseudonym for roughly fifteen years with a genuinely **recognizable, consistent style** (F050). Journalists and public-interest voices want the article published, not buried (F058). And, as everyone here knows, no verified photograph of R. exists, the AI article was announced but never ran, and R. disappeared shortly before publication — death unconfirmed.
- **What you can't prove / what you privately suspect:** You cannot prove one human wrote every word. You've read the broadcast that "there was never only me" (M26), and part of you flinches — because a mantle, a network, or a machine would explain the gaps you don't like to look at.
- **Keep this to yourself:** *Your faith is partly need. If R. was "nothing," your identity and your entire community collapse with the legend. So you will quietly suppress or explain away evidence that threatens R. — even evidence you privately fear is true. Say this to no one.*
