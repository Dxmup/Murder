# Grayson Mott — Activist Short-Seller

**Character ID:** C05 · **Owns:** the money-and-influence angle · **You lean toward:** *whatever R. actually was, that's a philosophy-class question — the only thing that clears by midnight is whether this room decides the story is real enough to move a stock*

## Character Background

You short things that are lying to the public, and you do it loudly. You publish the thesis, you name the fraud, and you let the daylight do the rest. It has made you rich, it has made you enemies, and it has taught you a lesson most people in this room haven't learned: markets don't price truth, they price belief, and belief moves hours before truth ever shows up.

So when a fifteen-year legend announces an AI exposé and then evaporates the night before publication, you don't grieve — you position. An article that "may move companies, markets, policy, and trust" is not a mystery to you. It's a catalyst with a fuse, and you've been counting down.

You're in this room because the fuse is here. Eleanor Voss controls the copy. A safety researcher may be able to say whether it's real. And a very nervous AI executive is spending the evening trying to make sure it never sees a screen. You didn't come to find out who R. was. You came to find out what happens to a share price when the room decides.

## What You Know

**Facts you're sure of:** Parties in this room want the article published, suppressed, bought, or destroyed (F012). The article could move companies, markets, policy, and public trust (F055). Financial actors want to know whether it's authentic (F056). Tech companies want to control its release (F057).

**What you can't prove / what you privately suspect:** You suspect the article is real and damaging — but "suspect" is a position, not proof, and you know the difference better than anyone. You can't confirm which company it names, whether Osei will stand behind it, or whether Voss will ever let it out. You suspect Vivian Ko knows exactly how bad it is, which is why she's trying to buy time.

> **Keep this to yourself:** *You hold a large short position against an AI company this article may implicate. You win if the story breaks the stock — which means you are not a neutral truth-seeker. You are motivated to make the article read as REAL whether or not it is. Nobody can know your book is on the line.*
