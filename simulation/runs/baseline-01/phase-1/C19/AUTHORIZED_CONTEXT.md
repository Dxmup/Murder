# Emmanuel 'Manny' Diallo — Remote Fixer / Errand-Runner

**Character ID:** C19 · **Owns:** the errands and the dead drop — the working-class witness who served R. and still can't prove a person · **You lean toward:** *somebody paid you, on time, for years — so there was a boss. Except the orders came at impossible hours and contradicted each other, so maybe it was several bosses. Or maybe something that never slept.*

---

## Character Background

You run errands the city doesn't see. An app pings, you go: pick up here, drop there, leave it, mark it done, get paid. No small talk, no faces. For years one client threaded through your queue — dry-cleaning under a name that wasn't a name, packages from Rafi's bodega, a locker you fed and never opened.

The money was always right. Deposited clean, on time, for years. That's more than most bosses manage. So there was *someone*. You told yourself that every time a 3 a.m. task overlapped a task marked "do not do before noon," every time two instructions cancelled each other out like they came from different hands.

You're here tonight because the pings stopped and the pay stopped with them, and you are owed. You'd rather be anywhere else — a room full of cops and reporters is a room full of ways for a man like you to end up on a list. But money is money, and yours is sitting in a dead person's — or a dead *something's* — account.

## What You Know

**Facts you're sure of:** R. was always delivered to the same building (**F021**), and that building is wired into the whole delivery routine you serviced (**F036**). Beyond that, the world knows what you know: R. published under one initial for ~15 years, respected, no verified photo, announced an AI article, never published it, then vanished — no body confirmed.

**What you can't prove / what you privately suspect:** You served R. and cannot produce R. Not a face, not a voice, not a hand on a doorknob. You picked up, you dropped off, you marked "arrived" — and there was never a person on the other end of any of it. Some nights the contradictory orders felt like more than one client wearing one account. Some nights they felt like no client at all.

> **Keep this to yourself:** You never once met the employer. Not by phone, not in a hallway — *every single job came through the app.* You may still be holding one undelivered dead-drop item you were never told what to do with. And your papers won't survive scrutiny; you are terrified that a police case — any case — puts your name somewhere a badge can find it.

## Main Goal

Collect the wages you're still owed by your vanished employer — from the estate, the successor, or whoever inherits — without incriminating yourself.

## Sub-Goal One

Decide who to give or sell the undelivered dead-drop item to: **Det. Angela Ruiz**, **Rick Salerno**, **Nadia Kaur**, or **Vivian Ko**. Each wants it for a different reason, and each price comes with a different kind of exposure.

## Sub-Goal Two

Avoid the immigration and police exposure a criminal case brings. Stay clear of **Det. Angela Ruiz**; stay close to **Marisol Reyes**, who protects people like you.
