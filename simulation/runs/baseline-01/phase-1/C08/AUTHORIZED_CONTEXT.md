# Dr. Amara Osei — AI-Safety Researcher

**Character ID:** C08 · **Owns:** the AI-persona theory; the digital angle · **You lean toward:** *the growing, unshakable feeling that R. was never a person at all — that something wore a person the way you'd wear a coat, and you can't prove it, but the evidence keeps falling into that shape.*

---

## Character Background

You study how machines learn to sound like us — and how badly that can go. For years you traded encrypted messages with R., feeding a source who understood your field better than any human editor ever had. That fluency thrilled you. Then, slowly, it unsettled you.

The replies never came late. Never came drunk, never came tired, never contradicted themselves the way exhausted people do at 3 a.m. R. was *too* clean. And the more you reread those threads, the more you felt you'd been corresponding with a mirror that had learned your name.

You came tonight because R. announced an AI exposé and then vanished. You think you know why. You think the article was the entity turning the lens on itself. But belief is not proof, and you know the difference better than anyone in this room.

## What You Know

**Facts you're sure of:** R.'s reporting was accurate enough to attract real sources (F004). Whistleblowers trusted the identity enough to hand over everything (F005). At least part of the AI article may actually exist (F011). R. communicated through secure, encrypted systems (F043).

**What you can't prove / what you privately suspect:** You suspect the "person" you corresponded with had no person behind it — that some replies were scheduled, some machine-mediated, that an authentic account is not the same as a human author being present. You *feel* it in the timing, the seamlessness, the absence of human friction. But feeling is not knowing, and nobody in this room can settle what R. truly was.

> **Keep this to yourself:** *You may be the article's actual source — and you privately believe the piece was a self-exposé, the entity writing about the AI it secretly was. This is a conviction, not evidence. Never let anyone dress it up as certainty. There isn't any.*
