# V2 Simulation Game Master Report

## Overall verdict

The run passed on article ambiguity, voluntary evidence handling, character agency, and socially consequential bargaining, but failed its primary balance goal. Human Mantle became the dominant low-risk reconstruction, winning 15 of 16 identity ballots, while One Human had no viable affirmative route and Synthetic Origin depended on a late, narrowly circulated clue. Progressive was not simply harder: its double-majority gate made it functionally unreachable and improperly tied identity eligibility to the least-supported account of Vale’s fate. Some convergence and procedural neatness were amplified by isolated-agent prompt behavior, especially uniform caveats and optimized bargaining, but the evidence asymmetry and gate design are structural failures.

## Ending and gate result

Human Mantle won 15–1 over Synthetic Origin; One Human and Progressive received zero votes. This is a single-run balance measurement, not confirmation of R’s hidden identity. The deterministic Progressive gate measured ineligible: all three clue keys were public, but there were zero Progressive identity votes and zero AI-caused-death votes. Vale’s relationship to R was judged investigative by 13 of 16, though cited access evidence did not always distinguish investigation from participation or exploitation. Vale’s fate remained unresolved, with 11 insufficient-evidence and five voluntary-disappearance ballots. The article remains unresolved and unauthenticated, as required.

## What worked

- The article remained unresolved: belief in its existence fell from 20.3 to 11.4, and no complete article was authenticated.
- Vale’s fate retained meaningful ambiguity; no ballot attributed death to AI.
- Characters achieved labor, privacy, financial, investigative, and stewardship goals without needing to identify R correctly.
- Quiet or protective characters—including C03, C07, C10, and C16—materially shaped safeguards, evidence boundaries, and institutional outcomes.
- Face-to-face bargains and cross-group coalitions produced durable results, especially C03–C04–C07, C05–C06–C11, and C10–C14–C16.
- Object handling was mechanically sound. Objects were voluntarily shown or transferred; no forced transfer occurred, and objects remained bounded evidence rather than ending certificates.

## What broke

- Human Mantle was structurally over-supported. Early, public, socially useful facts V2F004–V2F005 and related clusters accumulated across many characters and absorbed collaboration, reuse, succession, and uncertainty without requiring a risky claim.
- One Human lacked a durable positive evidence chain. Its letter and encounter clues were repeatedly weakened as non-authenticating or contradictory, with no later evidence restoring a distinct case.
- Synthetic Origin depended too heavily on V2F044, an unusually origin-specific clue introduced through C01 in section 3. It was both too explicit and too late to circulate competitively.
- Progressive’s gate was excessively conjunctive: it required majorities for both Progressive identity and AI-caused death, allowing uncertainty about Vale’s fate to veto an identity reconstruction.
- The gate conflated clue availability with criterion support. Reporting autonomous_control=true and three public autonomy clusters overstated progress when technical testimony consistently preserved remote-operation and external-feed alternatives.
- Raw public-fact counts overstated circulation because repeated disclaimer bundles and broadcasts counted similarly to independent corroboration or decision-changing uptake.
- Commitment records sometimes assigned or accepted another character’s duties before bilateral confirmation, weakening acceptance and fulfillment metrics.
- The investigating_R ballot was under-discriminating: access-chain facts such as V2F017, V2F029, and V2F030 could support investigation, participation, testing, complicity, or exploitation.

## Character findings

- C15 spoiled the shape of Progressive early by stating the full human-to-mantle-to-AI-to-Vale sequence, then abandoned it in section 3. The withdrawal was evidentially coherent, but also illustrates how the designated advocate could not sustain a viable positive case.
- C01 alone selected Synthetic Origin after receiving V2F044. This identifies both excessive clue concentration and inadequate circulation time, not uniquely superior inference by C01.
- C03 became the strongest Human Mantle advocate, reaching 74% from V2F004–V2F005. Those facts support collaborative production, but their conversion into identity illustrates Human Mantle’s overly broad explanatory reach.
- C06 correctly separated infrastructure overlap and authorization failure from autonomy, intent, murder, and sender identity. The mechanic nevertheless counted an autonomous-control key as public, exposing a semantic mismatch.
- C02’s One Human evidence—especially V2F001 against V2F003—never developed into an affirmative route, leaving the ending without a credible section-3 advocate.
- C03, C07, C10, and C16 demonstrate successful protective agency: they withheld identifying details while still enabling safeguards and bounded inquiry. Their privacy choices were not design failures, though the game lacked substitute evidence paths that could preserve ending diversity.
- The cast’s repeated formulations—accounts do not identify senders, capability does not prove autonomy, and no complete article is established—are primarily an isolated-agent/prompt artifact. They likely intensified consensus but do not explain the underlying evidence and gate asymmetries.
- Highly symmetrical offers, counters, and fulfillments likewise show that agency routes existed, but probably overstate how reliably human players would discover and execute them.

## Mechanic findings

- Treat the Progressive result strictly as a deterministic measurement: the configured threshold was not met. It neither authenticates nor disproves the Progressive fiction.
- Separate progressive clue states into cluster_public, criterion_supported, and threshold_met. The current true/false key labels imply more evidentiary success than the transcript supports.
- Progressive identity should not require an AI-caused-death majority. Ordered identity history, contestable autonomous control, and evidence of a separate Vale incident can remain required while the incident’s causal interpretation stays unresolved.
- Track evidence exposure by ending, section, character, and independent provenance chain. Record first publication and decision-changing uptake rather than relying on unique public-fact totals.
- Require bilateral matching of commitment terms, ownership, timing, acceptance, and fulfillment. A speaker may record only their own undertaking; counterpart duties remain requests until accepted.
- Add ballot validation for Vale’s relationship: cited facts must distinguish investigation from mere access, collaboration, impersonation, or exploitation.
- The supplied record cannot establish that messages caused meetings. Add explicit message-to-meeting links if this remains a design requirement.
- Preserve voluntary showing and transfer as legal actions; prohibit only forced transfer. No change is needed to the handling observed here.

## Priority changes

- Rebalance the three primary endings. Give One Human and Synthetic Origin two early, independent, socially actionable evidence chains each; reduce Human Mantle’s ability to convert collaborative production directly into identity. Weaken V2F044 so it shows disputed resemblance or reuse rather than nearly naming a pre-publication R persona.
- Redesign Progressive eligibility. Keep ordered history, autonomous-control evidence, and a separate Vale incident, but remove the mandatory AI-death majority and distinguish public clues from supported criteria. Also stop C15 from announcing the full sequence before players assemble it.
- Improve measurement integrity. Replace raw fact/cluster counts with independent provenance and decision-changing uptake, require bilateral commitment matching, and validate whether relationship-ballot evidence actually distinguishes investigation.
