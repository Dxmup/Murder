# Simulation Game Master Report

## Overall verdict

The simulation achieved robust mechanical integrity, character agency, and voluntary object exchange dynamics, but suffered from a severe consensus collapse toward 'human_mantle' (11/16 ballots) that undermined primary ending balance and structural weight parity.

## Ballot and ending results

Unresolved article status with heavy ballot convergence on human mantle (11), minor support for synthetic origin (2) and progressive (2), and minimal one human (1); Vale fate predominantly voluntarily vanished (10) or killed by AI (3).

## What worked

- Voluntary object showing (O01, O02, O03) operated cleanly without forced transfers, coercion, or mechanical exploits.
- Decentralized character agency and secondary subgoal pursuit (neighborhood coalitions, funding transparency, contributor anonymity) functioned exceptionally well.
- Progressive and alternative identity pathways maintained non-trivial engagement and genuine difficulty without defaulting to automatic consensus.

## What broke

- Premature ideological interlock between key actors caused 11 out of 16 characters to converge on 'human_mantle', violating structural weight parity across primary origins.
- Article existence belief means drifted upward (42-45%) rather than remaining anchored strictly at baseline ambiguity.
- Several character ballots (e.g., C01, C02) contained incomplete evidence chain citations and uncited evidence dimensions.

## Character findings

- C15 (Kit Rakes) championed the progressive trajectory across all three sections and anchored the progressive ballot position, but faced peer pushback and public retractions from C06 and C12.
- C03 and C07 established and maintained strict joint disclosure boundaries protecting anonymous Night Desk contributors.
- C01 and C05 successfully negotiated transparent funding terms and fulfilled commitments in Section 3, while C16 bound institutional actors to neighborhood resource agreements.

## Mechanic findings

- The deterministic ballot distribution effectively measured agent convergence and revealed systemic prompt priors favoring collective human authorship.
- Object custody and voluntary showing rules successfully preserved ending uncertainty without proof leaks or forced publication votes.
- Belief-update prompts permitted mild upward drift in article existence beliefs across sections 1 through 3.

## Priority changes

- Rebalance initial belief distribution anchors and prompt weightings to prevent premature convergence on human mantle.
- Introduce stricter divergence incentives and counter-evidence injection for synthetic origin and progressive hypotheses to enforce structural weight parity.
- Enforce rigorous validation checks during final ballot submission to ensure full evidence chain dimensions are cited.
