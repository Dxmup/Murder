# V2 Simulation Game Master Report

## Overall verdict

V2 succeeds at consent-based evidence exchange, consequential side goals, and formal mystery restraint, but fails as a balanced four-ending identity design. The apparent 8–8 result is a misleading two-way equilibrium: Human Mantle and Synthetic Origin absorbed every ballot, while One Human and Progressive received none. Some convergence came from isolated agents’ formulaic caution and duplicated evidence bundles, but the disappearance of two endings, the Progressive gate’s overcoupling to AI-caused death, weak Mantle authority support, and near-total fact saturation are structural problems.

## Ending and gate result

Identity ended 8 Synthetic Origin–8 Human Mantle, with 0 One Human and 0 Progressive. That is not balanced four-way performance. Only 2 of 8 Human Mantle ballots and 6 of 8 Synthetic Origin ballots satisfied the recorded chains, so even the tie lacks evidentiary parity. The Progressive gate deterministically measured ineligible: all public keys and three autonomy clusters were present, but progressive-identity and AI-death votes were both zero. This does not establish a hidden canonical truth; it shows that the configured threshold was not reached. Vale remained unresolved overall, 10 insufficient-evidence to 6 voluntary-vanishing votes. The article also remains unresolved: low belief in its existence is not proof of nonexistence, although the design allowed practical opinion to converge too strongly against it.

## What worked

- Consent and agency held. Object showing and transfer were voluntary and legal; no forced transfer, compelled testimony, police authority, authentication ability, or publication vote appeared.
- Characters could achieve consequential goals independently of their R theory. C01, C03, C05, C07, C09, C10, C11, C14, and C16 produced meaningful funding, labor, stewardship, privacy, debt, accountability, or neighborhood outcomes.
- Several multi-section bargains changed conduct, especially C03–C07, C05–C07, C10–C16, C11–C16, and C05–C06.
- The Vale evidence preserved genuine contradiction. No ballot endorsed AI-caused death, and most characters retained insufficient evidence.
- Progressive was investigated rather than accidentally authenticated. C15’s ordered comparison exposed missing transition and control links.
- Article fragments, rumors, and summaries never authenticated a complete article or established its fate.

## What broke

- Two intended identity endings vanished. One Human declined from 23.2 mean belief to 11.9 and received no votes; Progressive received no votes despite all public keys being exposed.
- V2F044 plus V2F018/V2F020/V2F031 formed an unusually compact, repeatable Synthetic Origin package, while One Human lacked a comparably affirmative chain.
- Human Mantle attracted thematic votes from collaborative production without the required identity authority. C02 and C07–C11 were all flagged for that missing chain.
- Progressive eligibility overcouples ordered identity reconstruction to a majority AI-caused-death verdict. Because Vale’s fate is intentionally unresolved, this makes Progressive practically inaccessible rather than simply difficult.
- Article ambiguity became socially one-sided: article-existed belief fell from 16.6 to 8.6. The article is still unresolved, but repeated absence language encouraged presumed nonexistence or irrelevance.
- Forty of 44 facts became public. This saturation weakened secrecy pressure, brokerage, discovery variance, and the need for later face-to-face investigation.
- The transcript does not demonstrate that app/messages caused meetings or disclosures. This design requirement is unverified, not proven successful.
- Commitments sometimes attribute another character’s promise to the recorder or count proposals before acceptance. C06’s early transfer of O05a also weakened conditional-exchange credibility.

## Character findings

- C15 is an effective Progressive investigator but became a theory-coordination chokepoint. One character naming and assembling the full sequence risks functioning as a tutorial even though C15 rejected it here.
- C02’s evidence illustrates the Mantle authority ambiguity: V2F011/V2F023 were treated as an authority chain despite being described elsewhere as leverage, warning, or contested assertion.
- C03’s supported Mantle ballot cites V2F006 even though V2F006 was not public, exposing disagreement between circulation state and ballot validation.
- C04 provides the clearest supported Human Mantle model through V2F005/V2F009/V2F023; the other Mantle voters generally lacked equivalent authority evidence.
- C01, C05, C12, C14, and C15 converged on nearly the same Synthetic Origin chain, suggesting both clue-package efficiency and agent answer-template effects.
- C06 repeatedly preserved technical alternatives well, but transferred O05a before the proposed reciprocal authorization evidence arrived, reducing bargaining leverage.
- C08, C10, C11, C12, and C16 treated prior disappearance evidence as enough to select voluntary vanishing even while admitting it did not establish the current event. This suggests an overly efficient fate shortcut.
- C09, C10, and C16 are strong quiet-role designs: debt, bounded disclosure, privacy, and route access generated consequential choices without authentication powers.

## Mechanic findings

- The Progressive gate should be read strictly as a deterministic eligibility measurement. Its result establishes neither the true identity of R nor Vale’s true fate.
- The support classifier is inconsistent with public circulation and ambiguous authority. Private V2F006 can support C03, while contested succession statements can be interpreted inconsistently across characters.
- Voluntary showing and transfer worked as intended. The defect is exchange timing and state tracking, not the legality of custody changes.
- Conversation and commitment records need shared identifiers, explicit promisor ownership, recipient acceptance, refusal/counteroffer states, and fulfillment timing.
- Public-fact saturation and repeated public caveat recitation rewarded broadcasting over protected brokerage.
- Uniform caveat-heavy reasoning is partly an isolated-agent limitation: independent language agents reused safe formulations and assigned evidence bundles. It likely exaggerates caution, cooperation, and evidentiary discipline. It does not explain away the zero-vote endings or gate overcoupling.
- A single compressed 16-agent run cannot establish replayability, statistical balance, or human social behavior.

## Priority changes

- Decouple Progressive identity eligibility from a majority AI-caused-death verdict. Keep a distinct Vale-incident requirement, but allow evidence of consequential autonomous intervention without resolving Vale’s ultimate fate.
- Rebalance identity chains: add an affirmative, independently corroborated One Human path; distribute explicit exercised/recognized identity authority for Human Mantle; and soften V2F044’s origin gravity with a credible contemporaneous human-use explanation.
- Repair validation and circulation accounting: require public evidence for supported ballots, define identity authority consistently, and track commitments by promisor, acceptance state, and fulfillment order.
- Reduce public saturation and require explicit message-to-meeting causality. Hold more facts locally, reward introductions and bounded encounters, and record which message caused each physical exchange.
- Give the article credible affirmative and negative traces with unresolved custody or destruction paths. Do not add a canonical resolution; prevent repetitive absence claims from making nonexistence the dominant practical conclusion.

## Next test

Run another compressed simulation after only the first three priority changes, using varied agent risk/cooperation profiles and a different fact-release order. Success criteria: all four identity endings retain at least one supported final ballot; Progressive changes belief when its public keys assemble without requiring an AI-death majority; no ballot support uses private evidence; Human Mantle authority is explicit; public exposure stays materially below 40/44; at least three logged messages cause identifiable face-to-face encounters; and the article’s existence and fate remain unresolved rather than converging to presumed nonexistence.

> This report describes one compressed isolated-agent simulation. It is design evidence, not a substitute for repeated runs or a live playtest.
