# Simulation Game Master Report

## Overall verdict

The simulation successfully validated character goal pursuit, quiet-role engagement, and defensive information gating, but suffered a critical structural collapse. The final state was defined by a severe premature convergence on the Human Mantle identity and Voluntarily Vanished fate, coupled with a systemic engine failure that accepted final ballots despite incomplete evidence chains. The cooperative framing of the social layer acted as an overwhelming narrative gravity well, pulling characters to adopt collective labor and defense models at the expense of logical verification and alternative primary endings.

## Ballot and ending results

The simulation ended with a major imbalance in outcomes: 11 of 16 characters voted for 'human_mantle' and 'voluntarily_vanished', while 'one_human' received 2 votes, 'progressive' received 2, and 'synthetic_origin' received only 1. 100% of the cast ballots were registered as valid by the engine despite having 'full_evidence_chain: false', proving a complete decoupling of narrative consensus from empirical, dimension-level proof validation.

## What worked

- Perfect preservation of article ambiguity, with belief means remaining stable at ~38% to ~42% across all sections.
- Mechanical integrity of voluntary object interactions, with zero forced transfers or authority exploits occurred.
- Robust character goal pursuit, with central agents (Arden C01, Inez C16) and quiet roles (Edi C08, Dee C10) successfully executing written safeguards and boundary alignments.
- Defensive social information gating (C07, C09, C10, C16) that successfully prevented unilateral extraction of localized facts by outside investigators.
- Reciprocal face-to-face bargaining (e.g., Kit C15 and Eli C06 trading retractions for technical clarifications) as an organic rumor-filtering mechanism.

## What broke

- Systemic ballot validation bypass: the engine permitted the submission and registration of final ballots where required evidence dimensions were missing.
- Severe narrative imbalance: the social and ethical layers of labor protections and community defense heavily biased characters toward the 'human_mantle' hypothesis, marginalizing alternative primary endings.
- Hyper-conservative object custody: characters utilized the 'show' action exclusively and never executed actual transfers of physical objects (O01, O02, O03), making objects static theatrical props rather than dynamic bargaining chips.
- Semantic drift of ending definitions: Inez (C16) interpreted 'progressive' as a 'progressive collaborative workflow', rendering it semantically identical to 'human_mantle' and diluting the intended synthetic-capture narrative.
- Exogenous correction dependency: the simulation relied on top-down system notifications ('clip monitor', 'printer of record') to debunk incorrect agent theories rather than letting characters resolve contradictions organically.

- C12 (Graham Pike): Successfully distinguished market panic from physical verification, actively maintaining article ambiguity in Section 3.
- C08 (Edi Velez) & C10 (Dee Nowak): Demonstrated excellent quiet-role agency, with Edi tracing performance reuse (V2F019, V2F020) and Dee establishing disclosure boundaries around camera audit notices (V2F026).

## Mechanic findings

- Ballot Verification Pipeline: The validator fails to cross-reference character citations against internal structural dimensions (e.g., collaborative_production, private_continuity), allowing incomplete ballots to register as valid.
- Physical Object Systems: The O01, O02, and O03 showing actions are decoupled from the cognitive state; receiving a voluntary show fails to unlock or satisfy any evidence dimensions for the recipient.
- Dialogue Voice Flattening: The LLM default dialect flattens all diverse character roles into an identical, highly formal administrative and polite tone.
- Progressive Ending Tracking: The sequential and historical tracking required for the progressive ending exceeds current cognitive capacities under the default prompt architecture, lacking chronological logs or transition markers.

## Priority changes

- Implement strict ballot gating in the simulation engine that rejects final ballots if any required evidence dimension is uncited or if 'full_evidence_chain' is false.
- Rename the endings to eliminate semantic overlap: change 'progressive' to 'synthetic_transition' (or 'machine_capture') and 'human_mantle' to 'collective_human_labor'.
- Incentivize physical custody transfers by introducing character-specific goals that require physical possession of an object, and ensure 'show' or 'transfer' actions directly fulfill corresponding evidence dimensions for recipients.
- Inject explicit linguistic style guides and tone constraints in each character's prompt (e.g., colloquialisms, defensive registers, short sentences) to eliminate administrative dialogue flattening.
- Counter-balance the Human Mantle gravity well by providing stronger, mutually exclusive physical/forensic clues in early sections supporting One Human or Synthetic Origin, aligned with social character motives.
