# V2 Simulation Game Master Report

## Overall verdict

The run is mechanically valid but structurally imbalanced. It preserved the locked ending, article uncertainty, Vale-fate uncertainty, voluntary object handling, broad agency, and useful social circulation. However, Progressive became an ambiguity-absorbing default rather than a demonstrably harder reconstruction: it won 14/16 identity ballots while autonomous control remained expressly unresolved. This single compressed run identifies credible design risks, not statistically robust balance results.

## Ending and gate result

No ending was authenticated. The deterministic Progressive gate was correctly ineligible: all three topics were marked public and Progressive received 14 identity votes, but AI-caused death received 0/16 votes. That is a measurement of the configured threshold, not proof that the three keys were substantively established. Vale’s fate remained unresolved at 16/16 insufficient-evidence ballots, and the article must and did remain unresolved.

## What worked

- Article ambiguity held: rumors, fragments, amplification, and provenance disputes never established a complete article.
- Vale’s fate remained genuinely unresolved; route overlap, automated intervention, unidentified access, and the missing endpoint did not become homicide proof.
- No object or message authenticated an ending. O01, O03, O04, O05/O07, O08, O09, and O10a retained meaningful interpretive or provenance limits.
- Consent mechanics worked. C03’s O02 transfer to C07 and C11’s O07a transfer to C05 were voluntary; voluntary showing and transfer are legal, and no forced transfer occurred.
- Characters could achieve consequential goals without solving R. Stewardship, worker protections, neighborhood representation, review, privacy, and accountability created independent agency.
- Messages and artifacts generated substantial interpersonal bargaining and evidence comparison across technical, labor, civic, journalistic, and neighborhood groups.

## What broke

- Primary identity balance failed in this run: Progressive received 14 ballots, Human Mantle 2, and One Human and Synthetic Origin none.
- Progressive absorbed rival evidence—human collaboration, mantle succession, technical reuse, and an unresolved Vale incident—without requiring an ordered transition or affirmative autonomous control.
- The key metric confused “raised publicly” with “established.” Characters claimed autonomous_control while explicitly preserving remote operators, external feeds, credential remapping, cloned automation, or falsified logs.
- C15 effectively exposed the three-key rubric, and repeated section-three phrasing likely caused AI-agent checklist convergence. This is partly a prompt/simulation artifact, but the design enabled it by exposing scoring vocabulary.
- Broad publication of 39 facts and dense summaries weakened information asymmetry and made some meetings feel like confirmation of already-broadcast material.
- Institutional bargains converged too smoothly. Corrections, caveats, disclosure, and safeguards became a dominant low-risk strategy with few refusals, costs, breaches, or incompatible demands.
- The 13/16 investigating-R result is weaker than its consensus suggests because evidence also supports contesting or reclaiming R; the current relationship option acts as another safe middle category.

## Character findings

- C15 is the clearest prompt-design problem: naming and repeatedly recruiting around the exact Progressive keys made the intended rubric salient.
- C06 became a technical interpretation chokepoint, despite correctly stating that historical autonomous control was unresolved.
- C13 and C14 became the principal Vale-route interpreters; their caution preserved uncertainty, but concentrated dependency in a small expert cluster.
- C03 was the most internally consistent ballot example: Human Mantle, only identity_history claimed, and explicit separation of collaboration from autonomy and Vale’s fate.
- C02, C06, and C15 illustrate key-claim inflation: their reasoning denied a clean causal chain or established autonomy while their ballots claimed all three keys.
- C03 and C09 show that Human Mantle and side-goal success remained playable, but One Human and Synthetic Origin lacked comparable late advocates.
- C01, C05, C07, C11, and C16 formed an effective remedy coalition, although its safe institutional agenda sometimes displaced mystery competition.
- C12’s admission of profitable amplification without authenticating a complete article successfully supported accountability while preserving the required unresolved article.

## Mechanic findings

- The Progressive gate’s final ineligibility is correct under its deterministic rule, but the key booleans are semantically unsound and should not be treated as hidden truth.
- Progressive identity and the locked Progressive-plus-AI-death ending currently have mismatched burdens. Agents can select Progressive while denying autonomy and AI causation.
- The AI-death threshold protected the ending here, but may be practically unreachable rather than merely difficult; repeated zero-vote runs would require separate review.
- One Human relies too heavily on C02/O01’s degraded witness-and-letter chain and lacks a recovery path.
- Synthetic Origin is mostly presented as a possibility to caveat, not an affirmative hypothesis with discriminating evidence.
- Human Mantle has affirmative collaborative-production evidence, but Progressive automatically subsumes it whenever technical participation appears.
- Conversation commitments are insufficiently stateful: later summaries often imply acceptance without a witnessed accept, reject, counteroffer, fulfillment, or breach event.
- O07a’s voluntary transfer was legal, but transferring it to Parallax-linked C05 weakened the stated goal of independent custody.

## Priority changes

- Replace Boolean Progressive keys with raised, supported, and established states. Require affirmative historical autonomy—not capability, unexplained activity, or topical discussion—for established autonomous_control.
- Narrow the Progressive identity ballot: require a cited, ordered chain of origin, transition, and later control. Add a mixed/uncertain option so Progressive is not the default “all of the above” answer.
- Remove exact gate labels and three-key language from C15 and section prompts; score role-specific observations externally.
- Add one independent affirmative chain and late advocate/payoff route for both One Human and Synthetic Origin, while preventing Progressive from automatically subsuming Human Mantle.
- Make exchanges and commitments explicit and costly: accept/reject/counter/defer states, selective rather than automatic publication, at least one incompatible demand or credible refusal, and neutral custody for independent-review objects.
