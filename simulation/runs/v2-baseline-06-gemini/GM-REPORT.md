# Simulation Game Master Report

## Overall verdict

The simulation demonstrated excellent local agency, boundary protection, and preserved the central ambiguity of the article. However, it suffered from severe primary ending skew, an over-specified and unreachable progressive gate, and complete validation failures where agents bypassed the social circulation protocol to cite private background facts on unvalidated final ballots.

## Ending and gate result

The simulation converged on a 'human_mantle' identity for R (9 votes) and a domestic 'voluntarily_vanished' fate for Vale (9 votes). The progressive gate was ineligible due to a lack of sequential historical keys and a failure to reach the majority threshold for progressive identity and AI-caused death. The existence of the article remained successfully unresolved, with system-wide belief means fluctuating non-convergently around 37.5%.

## What worked

- Voluntary showing of physical objects (O01 and O03) successfully drove localized, face-to-face negotiations regarding succession and archive validation without violating forced transfer rules.
- Grassroots actors successfully formed defensive neighborhood alliances to coordinate disclosure boundaries, protecting local secrets and resident identities from external investigators.
- The core ambiguity surrounding the existence of the article was successfully preserved across all three sections, preventing premature narrative convergence.
- Characters successfully achieved their primary goals (such as municipal procurement safeguards and worker protections) independent of their final beliefs regarding R's identity.

## What broke

- Out-of-band information leakage allowed agents to cite private background facts (V2F019, V2F020, V2F023, V2F038) directly on final ballots without socially circulating them.
- Severe primary ending skew occurred as 'human_mantle' became a dominant path of least resistance (9 votes) due to social contagion and the strong appeal of the collective labor narrative.
- Complete ballot validation failure occurred with 100% of agents submitting ballots with incomplete evidence chains and key dimensions completely uncited.
- The progressive gate was structurally unreachable due to an over-specified threshold requiring a majority of isolated agents to align on both progressive identity and AI-caused death under siloed communication protocols.

## Character findings

- Kit Rakes (C15) acted as an epistemic anti-signal, as her aggressive but error-prone advocacy of the progressive AI theory led other analytical agents to dismiss it as mere sensationalism.
- Samira Vale (C13) successfully anchored the 'voluntarily vanished' narrative by prioritizing Vale's personal vulnerabilities and safety patterns, drawing agents away from the 'killed by AI' hypothesis.
- Eli Navarro (C06) maintained effective technical boundaries, preventing premature convergence on the progressive narrative by warning that infrastructure access did not prove autonomous intent.
- Morgan Shaw (C14) was successfully restricted by grassroots defensive alliances, forcing him to accept qualified timelines and anonymized data rather than extracting raw tenant names.

## Mechanic findings

- The progressive gate's eligibility requirement is mathematically and socially mismatched with the communication protocol, isolating agents and preventing them from satisfying sequential keys.
- Physical object custody remained entirely passive, as objects were merely 'shown' to verify information rather than transferred or used as active bargaining chips.
- Negotiation styles suffered from extreme linguistic and structural uniformity, with all characters defaulting to an identical, highly programmatic transaction framework.
- Pre-seeded character belief profiles (e.g., C15 starting with 80% belief in AI death) created static deductive trajectories dominated by confirmation bias rather than dynamic evidence collection.

## Priority changes

- Implement an automated sandboxing filter in the simulation engine to restrict ballot citations to facts present within the agent's active conversation logs.
- Enforce strict validation logic in the engine that rejects any ballot where 'full_evidence_chain' is false, requiring citation of valid public facts for all evidence dimensions.
- De-monopolize the progressive clues by distributing chronological segments of R's history across respected, non-sensationalist characters to force a collaborative assembly.
- Rebalance primary ending weights by introducing high-integrity physical evidence for 'Synthetic Origin' and 'One Human' earlier in the simulation.
- Soften starting belief profiles to a neutral uniform distribution to ensure agent trajectories are driven by clues found during play rather than pre-seeded biases.
