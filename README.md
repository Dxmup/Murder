# How Many R’s in Murder?

An original, three-section immersive house-party mystery for 16 core players, with four optional roles. New York City, 2027.

Players inhabit their characters while moving freely through a real home staged as the anniversary venue. They mingle as they would at a party, pull one another aside for private conversations, carry physical objects between rooms, overhear only what proximity allows, and decide for themselves whom to trust. This is not a seated tabletop game or a sequence of moderated discussion rounds.

A celebrated investigative identity has published for fifteen years under the pseudonym R. A reporter named Vale investigates R and disappears shortly before an anniversary gathering at Veridian Dynamics. Rumors claim Vale or R was preparing an article about Parallax Systems, but nobody can establish that a complete article ever existed.

Players reconstruct three connected questions:

1. Who or what was R: one human, a human mantle, a synthetic persona, or a progression through all three?
2. What was Vale’s relationship to R?
3. What most likely happened to Vale?

The game has no hidden canonical solution. The host reveals the ending matching the room’s ballots. Facts support competing interpretations, and emotion, trust, fear, and persuasion may matter as much as evidence.
