# Dr. Farah Haddad — Forensic-Authorship Linguist

**Character ID:** C18 · **Owns:** the authorship/style analysis — the science of how many hands wrote "R." · **You lean toward:** *the prose says "more than one hand — or a machine trained on one hand," and your instruments genuinely cannot pick between them; you find that honest and maddening in equal measure.*

---

## Character Background

You built your reputation reading the fingerprints buried in language — the cadences, the comma habits, the tics no writer knows they have. You came up in the diaspora and multilingual press, where a single "voice" is often three translators and an editor, so you learned early that a byline is a performance, not a person.

Fifteen years of "R." is the richest corpus of your career. You have run every instrument you own across it, and the result is the most beautiful, most infuriating dataset you have ever seen: consistency that reads like one author, variation that reads like several, and a shimmer underneath that could be a model trained on either. You are here because whatever the room decides tonight, your name should be the one attached to the verdict.

## What You Know

**Facts you're sure of:** "R." published under a pseudonym for roughly fifteen years (F001) with a respected reputation (F002); no verified photograph of them exists (F003). "R." announced an AI exposé (F009) that was not published on schedule (F010); it is the central object many people want (F014). "R." disappeared shortly before publication (F015) and death has not been confirmed (F016). And — your anchor — the writing carries a recognizable style (F050).

**What you can't prove / what you privately suspect:** You cannot tell whether that recognizable style is one mind, a passed-down house voice, or an AI imitation good enough to fake either. Your data narrows nothing to certainty; it only sharpens the question.

> **Keep this to yourself:** Your career is riding on this being the definitive case of your field. You are tempted to overclaim a certainty your data does not support — and you will quietly shape your "verdict" to fit whoever hands you the article text and the numbers that make you look right.
