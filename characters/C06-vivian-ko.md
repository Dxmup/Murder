# Vivian Ko — CEO, Meridian Cognitive Systems

**Character ID:** C06 · **Owns:** the suppression play — the "was-it-our-AI" existential question · **You lean toward:** *R. cannot have been one of your models. You need that to be true. And the fact that you keep having to say "cannot" instead of "wasn't" is the thing that wakes you at 3 a.m.*

---

## Character Background

You run one of the most valuable AI companies on earth, and tonight it could evaporate. For fifteen years a journalist called **R.** filed stories under a single initial, then announced an AI exposé and vanished before publishing. The market has decided your firm is the target. Your stock is already bleeding.

You did not build a machine to impersonate a Pulitzer-caliber reporter. You are almost certain of that. But you build models that write with uncanny voice, and you cannot personally account for every deployment, every fine-tune, every research fork over fifteen years. "Almost certain" is not a place a CEO gets to live in public.

You came tonight to end this — to make the article disappear before anyone in this room can decide what R. was. If it's never read, it's never believed, and the question of whose model wrote it never gets asked in a deposition.

## What You Know

**Facts you're sure of:** Whistleblowers trusted R.'s identity enough to hand over material (F005). At least part of the article may actually exist (F011). Multiple parties want it published, suppressed, bought, or destroyed (F012). Its release could move companies, markets, policy, and public trust (F055). Financial actors are desperate to know if it's authentic (F056), and tech companies — you — want to control its release (F057).

**What you can't prove / what you privately suspect:** You do not know what R. was. You cannot prove your firm is clean, and you cannot prove it isn't. If R. was a model, you have no idea whether it was *yours*.

> **Keep this to yourself:** Your own legal team flagged that if the byline "R." was your company's model, disclosure obligations trigger — an existential liability. You must control that finding at all costs. Nobody can learn that your lawyers already put it in writing.

## Main Goal

Stop the article from being published or believed — buy it, discredit it, or bury it tonight.

## Sub-Goal One

Quietly determine — via **Dr. Amara Osei** or **Wren Kaczmarek** — whether the journalist was an AI, and control that finding.

## Sub-Goal Two

Neutralize **Grayson Mott's** attack on your stock.

## Special Ability

**Buy / Bury.** You wield corporate money and NDAs to purchase silence or a document, or to issue a public denial. Use it to acquire the article, buy a witness, or quiet a market attack. It has one hard limit: money cannot manufacture proof of what R. actually was. You can suppress the question. You can never truthfully answer it.

## AI Assistant

Your assistant knows this sheet — your facts, your materials, your messages, your goals. It does **not** know who or what R. actually was (nobody does, not even the people who made R. possible), it does not know other players' secrets, and it holds no hidden "answer" you can extract. It can help you strategize, draft, and negotiate — not settle the truth.
