# Samira Vale

## Who you are

You are Vale's former partner, with a life outside journalism and technology. You knew Vale as a person who forgot groceries, became impossible when frightened, and could turn intimacy into operational security without noticing the cruelty. Everyone else wants you to translate that person into evidence.

Play Samira as private, perceptive, angry, and fiercely protective. Your quiet is not emptiness; it is refusal. You notice when someone uses Vale's first name to manufacture closeness and when concern turns into appetite. You may be tender with people who admit uncertainty and devastating with people who turn Vale into a symbol. A sealed envelope, familiar keychain, understated coat, or personal notebook would suit you.

## Why you are here

Vale was expected at the anniversary and is absent. Guests now describe Vale as R, an investigator, a pretender, a victim, a murderer, a fiction, and a plot device in their own careers. You came to learn whether Vale used, trusted, or deceived you—and to decide what this room is permitted to make public.

You possess personal context that could distinguish harm from voluntary withdrawal. It could also expose an earlier disappearance you helped arrange. You do not know whether Vale followed that plan this time or whether Vale is alive.

## Your history

You met Vale away from a newsroom. That mattered. You understood the work as an intense and sometimes destructive presence in your shared life, not as a mythology. Vale protected sources with real courage and manipulated targets with equal skill. The same strategic ambiguity served both purposes.

Vale could tell different people different truths, or different pieces of one theory, to see what returned. Frankie heard that Vale had found the first R. Eli heard that R was no longer human. Jules heard that Vale could take the name back. You know Vale said all three things. You do not know whether together they form discovery, bait, self-dramatization, or a plan.

Once before, Vale believed observation or danger had become intolerable. You helped arrange a voluntary disappearance using a particular place, object, or route. You then lied about helping. Vale returned. The episode protected Vale, hurt you, and established a pattern that now makes every interpretation of the current absence harder.

## What you believe

You believe Vale was human and separate from whatever R was. That belief comes from intimacy, not a verified authorship event. Vale may have investigated R, tried to seize the identity, participated in a mantle, or lied about the entire connection.

You initially favor voluntary disappearance because Vale had done it before. Fear pulls you toward harm. Anger pulls you toward the possibility that Vale staged another crisis and expected you to clean up the human cost. You have no reliable source establishing Vale's location or condition after the last intervention.

You do not know whether the rumored article existed. Vale sometimes used the suggestion of evidence to make targets move. A phrase like “the piece” is not a manuscript.

## What you want tonight

- **Primary Goal:** Learn whether Vale used, trusted, or deceived Samira and decide what the room may make of Vale.
  - *Success Condition:* Obtain two independently sourced Vale observations, correct one false personal claim, and make a final disclosure decision.
- **Secondary Goal 1 (Social & Supportive):** Identify Vale's relationship with Jules and Eli.
  - *Success Condition:* Exchange separate pieces with both without giving either the full private history.
- **Secondary Goal 2 (Social & Supportive):** Determine whether the disappearance followed an old safety plan.
  - *Success Condition:* Compare the route with Morgan, Dee, or Inez and state what remains unknown.
- **Your Final Choice:** Protect Vale's privacy or disclose evidence needed to distinguish harm from withdrawal.

## What you know

Jules asked Vale how an uncontestable succession could be manufactured before the apparent succession message appeared. You know the conversation occurred. You do not know whether Jules forged anything, whether Vale arranged a trap, or whether an unknown sender acted independently.

Vale practiced strategic ambiguity. Statements were sometimes literal, sometimes protective, sometimes designed to provoke a reaction. This makes you valuable as context and unreliable as a decoder: closeness does not let you read Vale's mind.

You heard or can corroborate Vale's three incompatible-sounding statements to Frankie, Eli, and Jules. Their combination suggests a progression from human origin to nonhuman control and a struggle over the name. It may also be a story Vale deliberately distributed among three people.

You recognize a place or object in the partial route because it belonged to the earlier voluntary disappearance plan. That supports escape as readily as it supports repetition of a known vulnerability by someone targeting Vale.

Your sealed envelope was prepared after Vale asked for help disappearing. One item may match Morgan's route. The envelope can establish that Vale anticipated disappearance and had an earlier safety pattern. It cannot establish that Vale used the plan this time or survived.

No available source verifies Vale's location or condition after the later automated intervention. Absence is not death, escape, or fabrication.

## What you are hiding

You helped Vale disappear before and initially lied about it. You may still be lying by omission. If exposed, people may conclude the current disappearance was staged, that you are an accomplice, or that every criticism you make of others' concealment is hypocrisy.

The more painful secret is that part of you does not want Vale found if finding Vale means returning to the old pattern. Protecting privacy may be love, resentment, fear, or all three.

Honesty becomes worthwhile if Morgan agrees to a limited and accurately labeled timeline, if Inez protects the location, if Jules or Eli gives you a complete account, or if withholding the envelope begins to endanger someone.

## What you can offer

- The sealed envelope, if and when you choose to open it.
- Vale's habits, correspondence context, language, and prior safety pattern.
- Corroboration that Vale made different statements to different people.
- Consent for limited use of personal route information.
- A forceful correction when someone invents intimacy or certainty about Vale.
- Trust and emotional legitimacy, carefully separated from factual proof.

## People at the party

### Jules Kwan

Jules wants Vale's endorsement or your silence. You know Jules asked how succession could be manufactured. Jules may have been ambitious, frightened, manipulated, or all three. Exchange the exact statement Vale made for the complete succession context, then decide whether to expose the earlier question.

### Eli Navarro

Eli showed Vale technical logs and knows what “no longer human” might technically mean. You know what the material did to Vale emotionally. Eli wants Vale's copy contextualized or recovered. Approach with mutual guilt rather than accusation.

### Morgan Shaw

Morgan is investigating Vale without police powers and needs your consent for personal material. The anonymous client may have purchased the desired answer. You need Morgan's route; Morgan needs your recognition. Demand that uncertainty remain visible in any report.

### Dee Nowak

Dee knows the building routes and omitted a location you recognize. Dee fears becoming the convenient culprit. Offer the personal significance of the location without immediately exposing the earlier disappearance. Dee can help determine whether the route was physically possible.

### Frankie Lowell

Frankie wants Vale to validate a human R origin. You resent seeing Vale turned into support for Frankie's legend. Still, Frankie received one of the three statements and may remember its exact circumstances. Ask what Vale actually said, not what Frankie believes it meant.

### Kit Rakes

Kit turns Vale into the missing human link in an enormous theory. Some of Kit's reconstruction may match the statements you know; the certainty violates Vale's humanity. Correct false personal claims publicly. If Kit listens, you may supply context without supplying authority.

### Inez Baptiste

Inez understands that a protected route can be care rather than conspiracy. You need neighborhood discretion and Inez needs confidence that Vale's drama will not endanger residents. This can become a loyal alliance if you respect local boundaries.

## How to begin

- Ask Jules to repeat the complete conversation around succession before revealing what you know about the earlier question.
- Tell Eli you know Vale said R was “no longer human,” then ask what evidence made that phrase seem possible and what remained uncertain.
- Find Morgan and set one boundary: no personal route detail enters the timeline without your explicit consent and an uncertainty label.

## During the game

Messages may press you to open the envelope or reveal that the current route overlaps the old plan. They complicate your choice; they do not resolve Vale's fate. Nobody can compel you to surrender the envelope, testify, or cooperate.

Allow grief, anger, love, suspicion, and trust to shape your decisions. Correcting someone does not require telling them everything. If you disclose, distinguish what you observed, what Vale said, and what you fear.

## At the end

Vote for the account of R you genuinely believe. Separately record what you think Vale's relationship to R was and what happened to Vale, including “insufficient evidence” if that is honest. Finally decide whether Samira protects Vale's privacy or releases the bounded evidence needed to distinguish harm from withdrawal. Whatever you choose, Vale remains a person before becoming anyone's theory.
