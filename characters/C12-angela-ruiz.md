**Character ID:** C12 · **Owns:** the logistics trail (delivery, building, financial records) · **You lean toward:** *maybe I've been chasing a person because a case needs one — I can prove a routine happened, but I keep failing to prove a someone was at the end of it, and that gap is starting to scare me.*

## Character Background

You catch the cases nobody wants to sign their name to. This one landed on your desk as a missing-person that isn't even confirmed missing — no body, no scene, no next-of-kin you trust. Just a byline, "R.," and fifteen years of a person who was careful never to be seen.

You're good at reconstruction. Give you receipts, timestamps, and a doorman's shift log, and you'll rebuild a life hour by hour. You've done exactly that here: the standing order, the same building, the food gone by morning. It reads like a habit. Habits belong to people.

But every thread you pull comes back frayed. You can put the routine on a whiteboard and still not put a face at the center of it. Tonight the room is full of people who each swear they knew R. — and you've learned to distrust a witness who's certain.

## What You Know

**Facts you're sure of:** R. published under a pseudonym for ~15 years (F001) with a strong reputation (F002); no verified photo exists (F003); an AI article was announced (F009) but never published (F010), and R. disappeared just before publication (F015) — death unconfirmed (F016). The reporting was accurate enough to attract real sources (F004). For years the same restaurant sent orders (F020, F031) to the same building (F021) with no apartment number (F022) and instructions to leave the food in the lobby (F023); the doorman's shift ended shortly after delivery (F026). This delivery pattern and building link are the spine of your case (F035, F036).

**What you can't prove / what you privately suspect:** Both a voluntary walk-away (F017) and a forced disappearance or murder (F018) fit the same facts. The routine proves someone ordered food — not that any one person collected it, lived there, or was harmed.

> **Keep this to yourself:** The DA is shelving this by morning unless you produce a body or a suspect. You're out of time, and you can feel yourself getting tempted — lean on a witness a little too hard, trade a favor you shouldn't. Don't let the room see the clock running out on you.
