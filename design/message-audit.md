# Minimum Simulation Message Audit

## Scope

[`data/messages.csv`](data/messages.csv) is a compressed test schedule, not final production copy. It contains:

- three section-opening group messages;
- three additional room-wide framing or closing messages;
- two personal messages per core character, plus one controlled late rival-evidence beat each for Arden and Maren;
- forty messages total;
- no required in-app replies.

## Pacing

- Section 1: establish the party, article-rumor uncertainty, institutional stakes, and neighborhood protections.
- Section 2: put origin, production, embodiment, succession, and autonomous behavior into conflict.
- Section 3: assemble Vale's route and the post-contact intervention while preserving escape and human-interference alternatives.

Personal sends are staggered by at least three minutes in this compressed schedule. A live implementation may use wider intervals or fewer messages after testing.

## Guardrail checks

- `recipient` always names one character or `Everyone at Fifteen Years of R`.
- Displayed senders may be unreliable; recipient scope is never misleading.
- No message contains or promises a complete article.
- No message proves R's identity, Vale's role, murder, intent, or AI personhood.
- Technical messages explicitly separate capability, access, behavior, operator, and intent.
- Morgan receives an invitation mechanic, not compelled testimony.
- Kit receives visible correction of both deliberately false theories before the progressive assembly window.
- Every message names or strongly implies movement toward an in-person encounter somewhere in the party.

## Simulation hypotheses

1. Two personal beats per character are enough for a compressed agent simulation but may be too sparse for a three-hour live game.
2. The Section 2 public comparison of Vale's three statements may make the progressive sequence too obvious even though it does not prove the keys.
3. Eli still receives the crucial Section 3 bridge and may remain a funnel despite split logs.
4. The explicit statement that no complete article will appear should preserve ambiguity without making the article rumor feel irrelevant.
5. Kit's two corrections should reduce authority without removing the player's ability to recover credibility.
6. The late neighborhood evidence should generate bargaining rather than leave Inez and Dee waiting too long.

## Required evaluator questions

- Did each message cause the expected movement, private meeting, comparison, interruption, or disclosure?
- How many agents treated a message as proof despite its limitation language?
- Did any ending become nonviable before the final section?
- Could Lone Author survive E07 and the public three-statements message?
- Could Synthetic Persona survive the early paper letter and collaborative human labor?
- Did the progressive ending become eligible through genuine synthesis or merely because V2M024 stated the sequence?
- Did players continue debating the article's existence, or did the explicit absence of an artifact settle it as imaginary?
- Which characters could still pursue goals while withholding mystery information?
