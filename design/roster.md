# Character Roster (revised for diversity + framework balance)

**20 characters.** Revised from the original §9 list, which clustered ~11 of 20 on a single archetype (elite professional investigating a story) with everyone related to the journalist *transactionally*. This version organizes the cast by **stake and relationship**, spreads it across class / age / belief, and — the governing rule — keeps a living human **champion for every one of the four frameworks** so the central mystery never collapses.

## The four frameworks (must all stay plausible — §6, §19.13)

| Key | Possibility |
|---|---|
| **A** | **One real human** who fiercely protected their identity. |
| **B** | **A passed-down mantle** — the byline inherited by several people, *Dread Pirate Roberts* style. |
| **C** | **A rogue AI** faking a human, having planted years of breadcrumbs (deliveries, messages, style). |
| **D** | **Never existed** — an emergent myth assembled from unrelated anonymous activity and coincidence. |

## ⚠ Hard rule: no verifiable relationship with the human

> **No player character has a verifiable, in-person relationship with the human behind the byline.** Any provable human relationship would instantly kill Frameworks **C** and **D** — so it cannot exist.
>
> Every apparent personal tie is one of:
> - **Mediated** — contact was only remote/encrypted/financial/scheduled (texts, wires, voice notes, drops); could be a human, several humans, or an AI.
> - **Remembered** — a real memory retold so often it hardened into false certainty.
> - **Inherited** — the relationship was received secondhand, never firsthand.
> - **Fabricated** — the character is knowingly faking the relationship for leverage (money, fame, the estate, the article).
>
> The people who *feel* closest to the journalist are precisely the ones who **cannot prove a body ever existed.** This is the engine, not a bug — their contradictory claims are what force the room to decide what’s true.

## The 20

### People who *claim* the “journalist” as a person *(none verifiable — see hard rule)*
- **C01 — Claimed next-of-kin (“estranged sibling / adult child”).** Here to claim the body and the article rights. Last “contact” was wire transfers and cards — no in-person meeting in ~15 years. **Secret:** the kinship may be fabricated to seize the estate. Emotionally **champions A**, but is unreliable.
- **C02 — The “former lover.”** Publicly grieving a long romance. **Secret:** the affair was entirely *remote* and is at least partly *fabricated* for leverage; the “partner” could have been several people or an AI. Never verifiably met a human — which makes them the strongest engine for **C/D**.
- **C03 — The mentor who “discovered” the byline** ~15 yrs ago. Claims to have met the young writer. **Secret:** only ever received copy through a drop; the “meeting” is a *remembered* story retold into belief. **Champions B.**
- **C04 — The named successor.** Publicly anointed heir to the byline. **Secret:** the naming came as a *scheduled/automated* message — could be the journalist, the AI, or self-authored to grab the mantle. **Champions B.**

### Money & power *(collapsed 3 finance → 1; kept the stakes)*
- **C05 — Hedge-fund founder / activist short-seller.** Wants to know if the article moves markets. Framework-agnostic; follows money.
- **C06 — AI-company executive.** Wants the article suppressed. An AI-that-faked-a-journalist is existential for them — so they push *hard against* **C** (or quietly fear it).
- **C07 — Labor / gig-worker organizer.** Represents the people the AI exposé is *about*. Class counterweight; a moral, non-transactional stake.

### Tech *(collapsed 4 → 2)*
- **C08 — AI-safety researcher / whistleblower.** May be the actual source. **Champions C** (“a system could absolutely do this”).
- **C09 — Hacker / digital-forensics freelancer.** Device & account metadata — scheduled messages, changing device IDs. Evidence cuts toward **B or C**.

### Media *(collapsed 4 → 2)*
- **C10 — Editor-in-chief** who runs the byline now. Gatekeeper of the unpublished article — but note: even *they* only ever received copy remotely (no verified author). Institutional **A/B**.
- **C11 — Conspiracy livestreamer (the debunker).** Loud **champion of D** — “there was never a person; it’s a psyop, or it’s the AI.” *(Conspiracy theorist #1.)*

### State
- **C12 — NYPD detective.** The investigation. Wants **A/B** — a crime needs a victim. *(Federal prosecutor cut — redundant LE.)*
- **C13 — City councilwoman / political figure.** Wants to control the public narrative.

### Institutional NYC *(kept — they carry the delivery/doorman cluster and widen the class range)*
- **C14 — Restaurant owner.** Someone ordered food for years — but they never saw who ate it. Leans **A**.
- **C15 — Doorman / concierge / super.** Never actually saw anyone collect it. Leans **C/D**.
- **C16 — Delivery / gig driver (undocumented).** Mobile witness with real personal jeopardy — can “win” their game without solving anything (§7). Never saw the recipient.

### Belief, culture & community *(the other missing axis)*
- **C17 — Myth-keeper / online source-community moderator (the true believer).** Devoutly insists the journalist is real and heroic — on faith, not evidence. **Direct opposite of C11.** *(Conspiracy theorist #2.)*
- **C18 — Forensic-authorship / linguist expert** (promoted from document to PC; plausibly diaspora / multilingual-press background). Style analysis argues **B vs C** — keeps the mystery balanced.
- **C19 — Faith / mutual-aid community figure** (pastor / imam / rabbi, or a librarian the journalist “confided in” remotely). A different value system entirely: truth, forgiveness, protecting people over the scoop.
- **C20 — AI artist** who *collaborated with the “journalist” remotely* and believes it was an AI partner all along. **Champions C.**

## Framework coverage check

| Framework | Human champions |
|---|---|
| **A** — one real person | C01 claimed kin (unreliable), C14 restaurant owner, C16 driver, C12 detective |
| **B** — the mantle | C03 mentor, C04 successor, C18 authorship expert |
| **C** — rogue AI | C08 researcher, C20 AI artist, C09 metadata, C02 phantom “lover”; feared by C06 |
| **D** — never existed | C11 debunker, C15 non-witness, emergent-myth reading of the whole file |
| **Contrast pair** | C11 debunker ↔ C17 true believer |

**Note:** none of A’s champions can actually *prove* a human — they champion A on belief, memory, or claim. That’s deliberate: it keeps A alive without ever confirming it and closing off C/D.

## Diversity axes now covered
- **Stake / relationship:** claimed-kin, phantom-lover, mentor, successor, worker, believer, community — not only investigators.
- **Class & occupation:** hedge-fund/exec → organizer, restaurant owner, doorman, undocumented driver, librarian.
- **Belief & ideology:** AI-doomer, evangelist/exec, populist debunker, devout believer, labor-left, faith/community.
- **Age & background:** young successor → retired mentor; immigrant gig worker; diaspora authorship expert.
