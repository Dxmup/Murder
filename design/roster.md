# Character Roster (LOCKED)

**20 characters, cast locked.** Revised from the original §9 list (which clustered ~11 of 20 on a single archetype — elite professional investigating a story) into a cast organized by **stake and relationship**, spread across class / age / belief, with a living human **champion for every one of the four frameworks** so the central mystery never collapses.

## The four frameworks (must all stay plausible — §6, §19.13)

| Key | Possibility |
|---|---|
| **A** | **One real human** who fiercely protected their identity. |
| **B** | **A passed-down mantle** — the byline inherited by several people, *Dread Pirate Roberts* style. |
| **C** | **A rogue AI** faking a human, having planted years of breadcrumbs (deliveries, messages, style). |
| **D** | **Never existed** — an emergent myth assembled from unrelated anonymous activity and coincidence. |

## ⚠ Hard rule: no verifiable relationship with the human

> **No player character has a verifiable, in-person relationship with the human behind the byline.** Any provable human relationship would instantly kill Frameworks **C** and **D** — so it cannot exist.
>
> Every apparent personal tie is one of: **Mediated** (remote/encrypted/financial/scheduled contact only) · **Remembered** (a memory retold into false certainty) · **Inherited** (received secondhand) · **Fabricated** (knowingly faked for leverage).
>
> The people who *feel* closest to the journalist are precisely the ones who **cannot prove a body ever existed.** This is the engine, not a bug.

## The 20

### People who *claim* the “journalist” as a person *(none verifiable)*
- **C01 — Claimed next-of-kin (“estranged sibling / adult child”).** Here for the body and the article rights. Last “contact” was wire transfers and cards — no meeting in ~15 years. **Secret:** kinship may be fabricated to seize the estate. **Champions A** (unreliably).
- **C02 — The “former lover.”** Publicly grieving a long romance. **Secret:** the affair was entirely *remote* and partly *fabricated* for leverage; the “partner” could be several people or an AI. Strongest engine for **C/D**.
- **C03 — The mentor who “discovered” the byline** ~15 yrs ago. **Secret:** only ever received copy through a drop; the “meeting” is a *remembered* story. **Champions B.**
- **C04 — The named successor.** Publicly anointed heir. **Secret:** the naming came as a *scheduled/automated* message. **Champions B.**

### Money & power
- **C05 — Activist short-seller.** *(Locked.)* Holds a large short against an AI company the article may implicate — **profits if the story breaks the stock.** So they’re motivated to shape whether the article reads as “real,” *regardless of the truth*. An easy villain with a clean motive.
- **C06 — AI-company executive.** Wants the article suppressed. An AI-that-faked-a-journalist is existential for them — pushes *hard against* **C** (or quietly fears it).
- **C07 — Labor / gig-worker organizer.** Represents the people the AI exposé is *about*. Moral, non-transactional stake.

### Tech
- **C08 — AI-safety researcher / whistleblower.** May be the actual source. **Champions C.**
- **C09 — Hacker / digital-forensics freelancer.** Device & account metadata. Evidence cuts toward **B or C**.

### Media *(3 distinct modes — institutional, tabloid, fringe)*
- **C10 — Editor-in-chief** who runs the byline now. Gatekeeper of the article — but even *they* only received copy remotely. Institutional **A/B**.
- **C11 — Conspiracy livestreamer (the debunker).** Loud **champion of D**. *(Conspiracy theorist #1.)*
- **C16 — Tabloid / gossip reporter (TMZ-style).** Hunting the human-interest scoop; a **predator of the fabricators** (digs to verify or blow up C01/C02/C04). **Chases A**, but every faker they out feeds **C/D**. **Secret:** once ran a debunked “photo of the journalist” (PF10).

### State
- **C12 — NYPD detective.** The investigation. Wants **A/B** — a crime needs a victim.
- **C13 — City councilwoman / political figure.** Wants to control the public narrative.

### Institutional NYC & working world *(carry the delivery/building clusters and the class range)*
- **C14 — Restaurant owner.** Took the orders for years — *never saw who ate.* Leans **A**.
- **C15 — Doorman / concierge / super.** Never saw anyone collect the food; the regular face he *did* see was the fixer, not a resident. Leans **C/D**.
- **C19 — Remote “fixer” / errand-runner.** Years of app-dispatched pickups/drops/errands for the pseudonym; paid in transfers; **never met the employer.** **Champions A** (“someone paid me”) but **secret:** impossible hours / contradictory orders → suspects several bosses or a machine (**B/C**); may hold an undelivered dead-drop (PF17).
- **C20 — Bodega owner (near the anonymity building).** *(Locked.)* The block’s loud nerve center. The pseudonym kept a **cash tab** and had **packages held** here for years — a second, old-school anonymity node parallel to the restaurant/lobby. The **one witness who claims a face** — “of course I knew them, fifteen years” — except the details are mushy and *different people* settled the tab over time. **Champions A** loudly while the contradictions feed **B/D**; the odd-hour, auto-paid “hold this package” texts **smell automated** (a wrinkle back toward **C**). The loudest, *least reliable* witness.

### Belief & culture
- **C17 — Myth-keeper / online source-community moderator (the true believer).** Devoutly insists the journalist is real and heroic — on faith. **Opposite of C11.** *(Conspiracy theorist #2.)*
- **C18 — Forensic-authorship / linguist expert** (diaspora / multilingual-press background). Style analysis argues **B vs C**.
