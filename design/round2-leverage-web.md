# Round-2 Refit — The Leverage Web, Relational Goals & Dynamic Messages

> Driven by Playtest 02: the mystery works, but the **endgame has nowhere to fight** (once the arc is agreed, the sub-questions converge), **Eleanor is a goal-choking chokepoint**, and **too many players end “partial”**. The fix is one connected system.

## The one idea

Every character sits in a **web of inter-character leverage** — each **holds dirt** on someone and is **vulnerable** to someone. That web:
1. gives each character a **third goal** that is *relational and inter-group* (not mystery-solving, not routed through Eleanor),
2. makes the **messages rich**, because messages reveal *pieces of the web* with real specificity,
3. makes the game **dynamic**, because the web **detonates across the three acts** — an Act-1 claim gets recontextualized by an Act-3 reveal,
4. fixes **“nobody hangs”** — the erasure of the last R. can now be pinned on *named* people via the web.

> **Structural note:** this deliberately expands the §8 “main + 2 sub-goals” to **main + 2 sub + 1 relational**. Playtest 02 proved the game needs a win-condition that isn’t the mystery and isn’t Eleanor’s gate. Worth the added complexity.

## A. Eleanor rework + mechanical fixes

**Eleanor’s ability — was a chokepoint, now influence.** Replace *Publish/Hold (freeze a track + sole ownership gate)* with **“The Masthead”**: once per phase she may **publicly authenticate OR discredit one claim/document** — lending or denying the paper’s credibility. It *sways* the room; it **does not bind** it. **Byline/estate ownership is now decided by the endgame vote + evidence + the leverage web, not by Eleanor’s say-so.** She stays powerful (credibility is currency) without being the single gate every ownership goal dies at.

**The four Playtest-02 fixes:**
1. **Murder gets a convertible path.** The dead-drop / task #4471 / the locker becomes **forensically conclusive about the erasure IF** Ruiz secures a cooperating witness. Give the “runner wall” a real crack: **an immunity-flip** — Eddie, Rafi, or Manny can trade what they know for protection. So “who erased the last R.” *can* resolve to a named culprit if players work for it.
2. **De-chokepoint ownership** — via the Eleanor rework above **plus** the relational goals below, so Renata/Arthur/Nadia/Manny have ownership paths that don’t dead-end at her.
3. **Lockable goals** — a goal can be **banked** the moment it’s met (host/app marks it), not only at the buzzer, so a single endgame freeze can’t retroactively deny a dozen outcomes.
4. **Rick’s Print It** — auto-arms on **public** surfacing of Eleanor’s 7-year gap (unrebutted for a phase), not on her private concession, so it can’t be stonewalled dead. **Farah’s Expert Verdict** — the rival-expert rebuttal (Amara) is a **free, non-purchasable** action, and Farah’s stamp covers **one era only**, so authorship authority is contested, not laundered.

## D. Rich + Dynamic Messages (the 3-act reveal ladder)

**Principle:** every major web-thread unfolds across the acts — **seed (Act 1) → complication (Act 2) → detonation (Act 3)** — and each message is *specific*: dated events, named cross-implications, voice, stakes. A one-liner (“Nadia fabricated her way in”) is replaced by a paragraph that *implicates a chain of people* and forces a decision.

**Why dynamic:** the Act-1 version of a character is a claim; the Act-3 reveal recontextualizes it. What Nadia “is” in Act 1 (legitimate heir) is *rewritten* by Act 3 (a blackmailer who coerced her way in — and maybe erased the last R.). Third goals activate as the web surfaces.

### Flagship thread — NADIA (rewrites/expands the current M11/M15)
- **Act 1 seed → Nadia:** *“Congratulations on the byline. I remember when it was earned, not taken. —R.”*
- **Act 2 complication → Arthur:** *“She has your January email, Arthur — the one where you admitted the letter proves nothing after year three. She’ll publish it the moment you cross her. Ask yourself why she needs you quiet tonight. —a friend”*
- **Act 3 detonation → Nadia:** *“You blackmailed Arthur on January 12th — I have the copy of the email you sent. Then you threatened nullset with the video of her Rotterdam job. And now you’re coming for me. Do you actually believe you can carry this weight? The byline was never a crown, Nadia. It’s a debt. —R.”*
- **Effect:** recontextualizes Nadia’s legitimacy; arms Arthur’s and Wren’s relational goals; and “now you’re coming for me” makes Nadia a *named* suspect in the erasure — giving the murder axis a person.

### Flagship thread — AMARA ↔ VIVIAN
- **Act 1 → Amara:** *“They know you have it. Meridian’s security flagged your badge on the night the model’s logs were pulled. Move first. —a friend”*
- **Act 2 → Vivian:** *“The researcher is the leak. She was inside the model’s eval team in Q3. If she publishes, it isn’t a story — it’s a subpoena with your name on it.”* (internal security)
- **Act 3 → both:** *“The file names the model version. So does the file name Osei’s access. You can bury each other or bury it together. You have until midnight. —R.”*

### Flagship thread — THE RUNNER WALL (fixes the murder axis)
- **Act 1 → Eddie:** *“The twenty-three minutes didn’t delete themselves. Someone paid for that gap. You know who signed the tip envelope. —a friend”*
- **Act 2 → Ruiz:** *“The doorman is your crack. He didn’t lose the footage — he was paid to. Offer him the immunity and he’ll give you the night the last one vanished.”* (precinct)
- **Act 3 → Eddie:** *“Rafi already told them it was you at the desk that night. Talk first or wear it. —Ruiz”* (whether true or a bluff is the game)
