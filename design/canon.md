# Locked Canon

Running list of canonical decisions locked during design (things §3–§13 of the handoff left open). Everything here is fixed and propagates into character sheets, evidence, messages, and the app.

## ⚠ The game is FULLY INTERPRETIVE (locked)

**There is no hidden canonical truth about what R. was.** The designer does not secretly “know” whether R. was one human (A), a mantle (B), a rogue AI (C), or never a coherent entity (D). All four are *equally真* — the game is about how people construct truth from incomplete, contradictory, mediated information, and the answer is whatever the room decides at the vote (§6, §17).

**Writing constraints this imposes (binding on every character sheet, message, and evidence item):**
1. **No document, fact, secret, or private note may resolve R.’s nature.** A character may *believe* fiercely (C08’s self-exposé belief, C17’s faith, C01’s kinship) — but every such belief is labeled and held as *belief/claim*, never as verified fact.
2. **Secrets are facts about the CHARACTER, not about R.** Legitimate: “Dorian knows he fabricated the affair.” Illegitimate: “Dorian knows R. was really a person named …” — because *no one knows that*, including the designer.
3. **The AI assistants (§12) contain no solution** — there is none to leak. They help a character organize their own view; they never adjudicate the mystery.
4. **The endgame ballot is genuinely open.** No “correct” answer; scoring rewards persuasion, goals completed, and support-for-your-read — not being “right” (there is no right).
5. When writing a sheet, the author must **not** privately decide the answer and seed toward it. Write each character as a sincere, partial witness to a truth nobody holds whole.

> This is the keystone that makes the four-framework balance *safe*: since nothing resolves R., no leak can “spoil” a fixed answer — the only failure is a fact/message that makes one framework trivially unarguable (guarded by Rules 1–2 below and the pressure-tests).

## ⚠ Hard rules (from pressure-test rounds 1–2)

**Rule 1 — no proof of a single living human.** No character's fact-set, nor any plausible pooling, may *prove* one real person existed — that would kill C and D. Every apparent personal tie is mediated / remembered / inherited / fabricated.

**Rule 2 — no proof of coherent single authorship.** No artifact or message may *prove* a single coherent author produced the body of work — that would kill D. Consequence: the unpublished article, whenever shown, is **fragmentary, multi-voice, disputed/possibly-machine authorship** — never a clean single-author manuscript (message M40; ties F011/F053).

## The byline: **“R.”**
The journalist publishes under the single-initial byline **“R.”** — pieces read *“by R.”*
- **Why:** a byline that is almost *nothing* — maximally Satoshi/Dread-Pirate-Roberts hollow, impossible to attach to a face (reinforces F003 no-photo; keeps A/B/C/D all live). Echoes the title, *How Many R's in Murder?*, and the theme of disputed simple answers.
- **Usage:** every reference to the journalist’s public identity uses “R.” Characters may speculate what the “R” stands for (a name? “reporter”? nothing?) — never resolved.
- **Community/lore hook:** C17’s community (“The Questionry”) and the charity donations signed *“Questions matter”* (PF09) orbit the mystique of “R.”

## The article artifact
Canonically **never a clean manuscript** (Rule 2). When surfaced (M40) it is fragments — more than one hand in the prose, some passages possibly model-generated, authenticity disputed.
