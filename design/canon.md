# Locked Canon

Running list of canonical decisions locked during design (things §3–§13 of the handoff left open). Everything here is fixed and propagates into character sheets, evidence, messages, and the app.

## ⚠ Hard rules (extended after pressure-test round 2)

**Rule 1 — no proof of a single living human.** No character's fact-set, nor any plausible pooling, may *prove* one real person existed — that would kill Frameworks C (rogue AI) and D (never existed). Every apparent personal tie is mediated / remembered / inherited / fabricated.

**Rule 2 (NEW) — no proof of coherent single authorship.** No artifact or message may *prove* that a single coherent author produced the body of work — that would kill Framework D specifically. Round 2 caught this: handing the room a clean, single-voice `article.pdf` at the endgame quietly refuted “no coherent entity ever existed.”
- **Consequence, now canon:** the unpublished article, whenever shown, is **fragmentary, multi-voice, and of disputed/possibly-machine authorship** — never a clean single-author manuscript. (See message M40; ties to F011 “part may exist” and F053 “model prose could explain it.”)

## The byline: **“R.”**
The journalist publishes under the single-initial byline **“R.”** — pieces read *“by R.”*
- **Why:** a byline that is almost *nothing* — maximally Satoshi/Dread-Pirate-Roberts hollow, impossible to attach to a face (reinforces F003 no-photo; keeps A/B/C/D all live). Echoes the game's title, *How Many R's in Murder?*, and the theme of disputed simple answers.
- **Usage:** every reference to the journalist's public identity uses “R.” Characters may speculate what the “R” stands for (a name? “reporter”? nothing?) — never resolved.
- **Community/lore hook:** C17's community (“The Questionry”) and the charity donations signed *“Questions matter”* (PF09) orbit the mystique of “R.”

## The article artifact
Canonically **never a clean manuscript** (see Rule 2). When surfaced (M40) it is a set of fragments — more than one hand in the prose, some passages possibly model-generated, authenticity disputed. This preserves all four frameworks at the endgame vote.
