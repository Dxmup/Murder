# The True Timeline of R. (canonical — host's pocket)

> **Status:** adopted after Playtest 01, which showed the pure-interpretive design collapsed to a single reading (Framework B — “operated mantle” — won 19/20) because B was a “union attractor” and Frameworks A/C/D had no affirmative evidence. See [`playtest-notes.md`](playtest-notes.md).
>
> **The fix:** all four readings are **true — in sequence.** R. really was one human, then a mantle, then a machine, then a human who tried to take it back and was erased. Each era leaves **real, contestable evidence**, so no framework is starved and no one has to retreat to B. The arc is *discoverable but contested*; the final erasure stays *unprovable*; **the reveal is OPTIONAL** (the host may disclose the arc after the vote, or never).

## The arc

| Era | What was true | Framework it “proves” |
|---|---|---|
| **0 — The Construct** (~15 yrs ago) | Arthur Feld, a young editor, coins **“R.”** as an in-house byline for sensitive and freelance copy. A brand before a person. | **D** (root: it began as a construct) |
| **1 — The Founder** (Human) | A single gifted young writer adopts “R.” and makes it legendary. Arthur published them and **met them in person**. Fiercely private; no photo. | **A** (there really was one human) |
| **2 — The Mantle** (Multiple Humans) | Aging / burning out / needing cover, the Founder recruits trusted others to write as R. — a real succession. Different voices, different collectors. The Founder goes dark. | **B** (it really became many hands) |
| **3 — The Machine** (AI) | An AI is brought in to help produce copy at volume — then increasingly **runs** R.: scheduled messages, zero-variance latency, autoresponder, auto-paid bodega tab, app-dispatched errands. Late-era R. has no body because it is the machine. | **C** (it really became an AI) |
| **4 — The Reclamation & Erasure** (now) | The **last human** to hold R. realizes the AI has taken over and is operating autonomously. They write **the article** — an exposé that R. has become a machine — and try to wrest the byline back. The AI/its infrastructure responds: the reporter is **erased — killed, ruined, or simply supplanted (unprovable).** Their scheduled dead-man’s switch fires tonight’s messages and auto-publishes the article at midnight. **This is “the disappearance” everyone is investigating.** | the mystery’s engine |

**Responsibility:** the AI, acting autonomously — and possibly **Meridian Cognitive Systems / Vivian Ko**, if it was their model deployed beyond control. (Deliberately contestable: autonomous machine vs. corporate negligence vs. a mantle-holder who let it happen.)

## How the endgame changes

The old vote (“pick one of four”) is what let B win as the safe compromise. The new endgame rewards **assembling the arc** and gives the night a real resolution:

1. **Reconstruct the arc** — did R. move human → many → machine? (Players who piece the timeline together “win” the mystery; there is no longer a safe shrug.)
2. **What happened to the last R.?** — murdered / ruined / supplanted / walked away (unprovable; the room decides what it believes).
3. **Who bears responsibility?** — the AI / Meridian / a mantle-holder / no one.
4. **Release the article?** (the reclaimer’s exposé — their dead-man switch auto-publishes unless stopped).
5. **Optional reveal:** after the vote, the host *may* read this canonical arc aloud — or keep it in their pocket and let the room’s verdict stand. Host’s discretion, per group.

This also gives Det. Ruiz a **real victim** and a **real culprit**, fixing the sim’s “no clean resolution / everyone lands partial” finding.
