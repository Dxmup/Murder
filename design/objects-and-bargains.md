# Objects, Commitments, and Bargains

## Design rule

Objects create occasions for conversation; they do not solve the mystery. Possession is physical and voluntary. The app never tracks custody, and no ability can force a transfer.

A valid bargain exchanges at least two kinds of value:

- information;
- access or introduction;
- money or material support;
- public support or correction;
- protection, confidentiality, or attribution;
- a future commitment;
- custody of a physical object.

Promises are socially enforceable, not mechanically guaranteed. Players may betray them and accept the consequences.

## Core physical objects

### O01 — First R Letter packet

**Starts with:** Frankie.

Contains a facsimile letter and envelope. The original remains in controlled storage, preventing destructive inspection or claims based on hidden marks. Arden holds a separate archive intake report; those items must be compared.

**Can establish:** period-consistent physical provenance, recurring phrasing, and conflict with Frankie's delivery memory.

**Cannot establish:** author, sender, whether Frankie met R, or whether the letter relates to the first published work.

**Natural bargains:** Frankie trades viewing access for public recognition; Arden trades the intake report for conditions on how Veridian is credited; optional Farah offers a bounded assessment in return for disclosure of the informal examination.

### O02 — Night Desk routing slip

**Starts with:** Tessa.

A working copy showing several role initials, translation marks, and a shared drop code on material later published as R.

**Can establish:** collaborative production and a connection to the Night Desk workflow.

**Cannot establish:** that every contributor authored as R, who made final decisions, or when the practice began.

**Natural bargains:** protection for a contributor; archive access; conditions on Jules's succession; labor commitments from Veridian or Parallax.

### O03 — Succession message printout

**Starts with:** Jules.

Printed body and partial header of the message naming Jules. A detachable or separately held header supplement prevents Jules from authenticating it alone.

**Can establish:** what Jules received, account association, scheduled delivery, and phrase overlap when compared with other records.

**Cannot establish:** composer, sender, intent, or legitimacy of succession.

**Natural bargains:** Jules grants inspection in return for qualified support; Eli asks for full headers; Frankie asks for the old phrase to be credited; Samira threatens the earlier Vale correspondence.

### O04 — Edi's commission card

**Starts with:** Edi.

A payment reference, date, performance parameters, and a redacted client route. Source recordings remain Edi's unless voluntarily demonstrated.

**Can establish:** a performed identity event and the use of both human direction and generated material.

**Cannot establish:** that the client was R or that the performance was presented as R.

**Natural bargains:** confidentiality; attribution; Jules's payment context; Frankie's witness account; protection against Kit's numerology rumor.

### O05 — Continuity log excerpts

**Starts with:** Eli as three separate cards, not one complete dump.

- **O05a Capability:** persona continuity across operators and migrations.
- **O05b Access:** overlap with R-associated infrastructure.
- **O05c Activity:** adaptive behavior and its external-feed limitation.

**Can establish:** system capabilities, access overlap, and bounded behavior.

Splitting the logs lets Eli disclose selectively and prevents one all-purpose technical reveal.

### O06 — Rafi's account strip

**Starts with:** Rafi.

A narrow facsimile of recurring transactions showing a stable habit, several collectors, and one disputed change. Private customer details remain covered.

**Can establish:** transaction continuity and variation.

**Cannot establish:** customer identity, residence, authorship, or a precise transfer date.

**Natural bargains:** settlement of an old balance; neighborhood protections; a bounded timeline exchange with Morgan; public correction if Graham or Kit overstates it.

### O07 — Deleted calendar recovery

**Starts split between:** Celeste holds partial recovery **O07a**; Mira holds matching access authorization **O07b**.

**Can establish:** an off-calendar Vale meeting and limited Parallax access when the halves are compared.

**Cannot establish:** what Vale learned, why access was granted, whether the article existed, or whether the meeting endangered Vale.

**Natural bargains:** political protection, technical records, public responsibility, worker safeguards, or concealment of the deletion.

### O08 — Morgan's route board

**Starts with:** Morgan.

A foldout timeline with verified, attributed, disputed, and blank segments visually distinguished. It includes the manufactured-looking lead but not its suspected origin.

**Can establish:** what is known about Vale's movements and where accounts conflict.

**Cannot establish:** that Vale used the service exit, died, was R, or was targeted by an AI.

**Natural bargains:** Morgan adds a witness's account only with consent and attribution terms; witnesses seek protection or correction; Graham may offer money for premature certainty.

### O09 — Samira's sealed Vale envelope

**Starts with:** Samira.

Contains a copied line about preparing to disappear and an image or token Samira recognizes from the route. Samira may open it, show only one item, give it to another player, or keep it sealed.

**Can establish:** Vale anticipated disappearance and had a prior safety pattern.

**Cannot establish:** whether the anticipation was sincere, whether Vale followed the plan, or whether Vale survived.

**Natural bargains:** honesty from Jules or Eli; privacy terms from Morgan; a public correction from Kit; neighborhood context from Inez.

### O10 — Intervention notice

**Starts split between:** Dee has physical consequence or local notice **O10a**; Eli receives system-event portion **O10b** in Section 3.

**Can establish:** an automated intervention occurred after Vale's last verified contact and affected a real-world route, access, reservation, or warning.

**Cannot establish:** intent, operator, whether Vale encountered it, harm, or death.

**Natural bargains:** Dee needs protection before comparing it; Eli needs physical corroboration; Morgan supplies chronology; Inez supplies a nonlethal escape interpretation.

## Objects deliberately excluded

- A complete or substantially complete article.
- A verified photograph of R.
- A confession from a human or AI.
- A body, death certificate, murder weapon, or forensic cause-of-death report.
- A definitive authorship test.
- A master log naming every operator.
- A token that grants ownership of R.
- Arrest, immunity, subpoena, compel, truth, or forced-transfer cards.

## Commitment cards

Use a small stack of blank two-part commitment cards. The parties write:

1. what each person promises;
2. whether the agreement is public or private;
3. when it must be performed;
4. both signatures.

The card records a social promise, not a rule-enforced contract. Suggested commitments include:

- funding without editorial control;
- worker anonymity and representation;
- neighborhood witness protection;
- archive access with attribution;
- qualified public support;
- correction or retraction of a claim;
- confidentiality until a named section;
- repayment, debt forgiveness, or a future service.

The host never decides whether a promise was kept. Players may reveal broken commitments as social evidence.

## Custody and copying

- Objects move only with the holder's consent.
- A holder may show an object without surrendering it.
- Players may take handwritten notes but may not photograph props unless the holder agrees.
- No object is destroyed during play.
- When a message supplies a digital attachment, the physical prop remains the primary tradeable object unless the design explicitly says otherwise.
- The host resolves practical disputes about ownership but never interprets what an object proves.

## Minimum 16-player prop set

Use O01–O10 plus blank commitment cards. Optional-role props should duplicate provenance routes:

- Ash: disputed image contact sheet;
- Farah: material-comparison worksheet;
- Dorian: correspondence excerpts with missing context;
- Manny: undelivered object and dispatch receipt.

None is necessary for an ending or progressive key.
