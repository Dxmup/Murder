# Objects, Commitments, and Bargains

## Design rule

Objects create occasions for conversation; they do not solve the mystery. Possession is physical and voluntary. The app never tracks custody, and no ability can force a transfer.

A valid bargain exchanges at least two kinds of value:

- information;
- access or introduction;
- money or material support;
- public support or correction;
- protection, confidentiality, or attribution;
- a future commitment;
- custody of a physical object.

Promises are socially enforceable, not mechanically guaranteed. Players may betray them and accept the consequences.

## Core physical objects

**Starts with:** Frankie.

Contains a facsimile letter and envelope. The original remains in controlled storage, preventing destructive inspection or claims based on hidden marks. Arden holds a separate archive intake report; those items must be compared.

**Natural bargains:** Frankie trades viewing access for public recognition; Arden trades the intake report for conditions on how Veridian is credited; optional Farah offers a bounded assessment in return for disclosure of the informal examination.

## Objects deliberately excluded

- A complete or substantially complete article.
- A verified photograph of R.
- A confession from a human or AI.
- A body, death certificate, murder weapon, or forensic cause-of-death report.
- A definitive authorship test.
- A master log naming every operator.
- A token that grants ownership of R.
- Arrest, immunity, subpoena, compel, truth, or forced-transfer cards.

## Commitment cards

Use a small stack of blank two-part commitment cards. The parties write:

1. what each person promises;
2. whether the agreement is public or private;
3. when it must be performed;
4. both signatures.

The card records a social promise, not a rule-enforced contract. Suggested commitments include:

- funding without editorial control;
- worker anonymity and representation;
- neighborhood witness protection;
- archive access with attribution;
- qualified public support;
- correction or retraction of a claim;
- confidentiality until a named section;
- repayment, debt forgiveness, or a future service.

The host never decides whether a promise was kept. Players may reveal broken commitments as social evidence.

## Custody and copying

- Objects move only with the holder's consent.
- A holder may show an object without surrendering it.
- When an object is transferred, the person transmitting the item retains memory of that item, but only so far as knowing exactly which person they handed the item to.
- Players may take handwritten notes but may not photograph props unless the holder agrees.
- No object is destroyed during play.
- When a message supplies a digital attachment, the physical prop remains the primary tradeable object unless the design explicitly says otherwise.
- The host resolves practical disputes about ownership but never interprets what an object proves.

## Minimum 16-player prop set

Use O01–O14 plus blank commitment cards. Optional-role props should duplicate provenance routes:

- Ash: disputed image contact sheet;
- Farah: material-comparison worksheet;
- Dorian: correspondence excerpts with missing context;
- Manny: undelivered object and dispatch receipt.

None is necessary for an ending or progressive key.
