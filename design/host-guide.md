# Host / GM Run Guide — *How Many R's in Murder?*

Everything you need to run the three-hour game for ~20 players. Read this end-to-end once before your first run. Companion data: [`data/messages.csv`](data/messages.csv) (the schedule), [`data/characters.csv`](data/characters.csv), the sheets in [`/characters`](../characters), [`data/evidence.csv`](data/evidence.csv), [`canon.md`](canon.md), and [`canon-timeline.md`](canon-timeline.md) (the host's map of competing reconstructions and epistemic guardrails).

---

## 0. The host's job (and the one rule)

There is **no host-only answer** to whether R. was ever human. One human, a human mantle, a synthetic persona from the beginning, and an institutional construct must remain live through the final vote ([`canon-timeline.md`](canon-timeline.md)). **You hold the uncertainty, not a solution.** Each player is a sincere but limited witness. Your job is three things:

1. **Timing** — release the right message at the right moment so the room keeps moving.
2. **Momentum** — notice who's stuck and feed them a thread.
3. **Safety** — keep the *social* game safe even while the *fictional* game gets cutthroat.

> **The one rule you must never break:** never confirm any reading. When asked “so what really happened?” your answer is *“The evidence supports more than one complete reconstruction; the room decides what it believes.”* There is no canonical solution reveal after the vote.

## 2. Safety & tone (do not skip)

This is a game of **deception, accusation, and exposure**, and several characters carry real-world-sensitive material: undocumented status (Nikos's cook, Manny, Marisol's people), a fabricated romance, financial/political corruption, and existential AI/identity themes. Run it with a light safety scaffold:

- **Opening brief (2 min):** “Everything tonight is fiction. Your character may lie, betray, and accuse — *you* and the other player are on the same team. Play the character, respect the person.”
- **Cut / Brake:** teach two words. **“Cut”** = stop the scene now. **“Brake”** = keep going but dial this down. Anyone can use either, no justification needed.
- **Check-in gesture:** a discreet thumbs-tilt means “you good?”
- **Off-game corner:** a marked space where anyone can step fully out of character.
- **Sensitive-theme note:** tell players up front that immigration status, surveillance, and betrayal appear in the fiction; invite anyone to flag a line to route around.
- **De-role at the end (§8).**

## 3. Structure at a glance

Three ~55-minute phases, each opened by a public R. broadcast, plus a ~10-min endgame. The phases deepen the question without tracing a canonical answer: the apparent disappearance → competing identity histories → whether there was ever a human journalist or a murder at all.

| Phase | Question | Feel | Opens with |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** (0:00–0:55) | *Where is R.?* | Orientation, alliances, the routine | **M01** (R. to all) |
| **2** (1:00–1:58) | *Who is R.?* | Contradictions, the identity fight | **M10** (wire: “maybe not one person”) |
| **3** (2:00–2:52) | *Was there ever a human R.?* | Destabilize, deal-making, panic | **M26** (R.: “never only me”) |
| **End** (2:52–3:00) | *The vote* | Defend a reconstruction; publish-or-not | **M40** (disputed scheduled message) |

## 5. Adjudicating the eight abilities

Only eight characters have a mechanic. **Golden rule: no ability resolves what R. was.**

| Character | Ability | How you rule it |
|---|---|---|
| Eleanor (C10) | **Publish / Hold** | Controls the article's fate; can **freeze ONE track at a time** (one person's publish/leak/sale) — *not* the room. The M40 vote can override her. (Fixed after Playtest 01: the old version froze everyone in Phase 1.) |
| Wren (C09) | **Forensics** | Once/phase, a verdict on **one message/device's** authenticity or origin. You feed her the *provenance* — never “R. was X.” She may lie to a buyer. |
| Ruiz (C12) | **Compel + Narrow Immunity** | Compel once for one physical document. Separately, offer one witness immunity for one named offense in exchange for first-hand testimony. Neither authenticates identity; immunized testimony remains contestable. |
| Vivian (C06) | **Buy / Bury** | Meridian money/NDAs: purchase silence or a prop, or issue the denial (M37). Track deals; binding in-fiction. The model ability — it *preserves* ambiguity. |
| Grayson (C05) | **Leverage** | Binding financial offers. Money buys what someone *says*, never the truth. |
| Rick (C16) | **Print It** | Push a story to the public feed (lands as “fact” even if false). **Forcing trigger:** if he's uncovered Eleanor's 7-year gap by Phase 3 and she hasn't given him an exclusive, he may auto-print it. |
| Cody (C11) | **Go Live** | Broadcast an unverified claim to the whole room. Momentum tool; let it ripple. |
| Farah (C18) | **Expert Verdict** | She declares only **how many hands** (one/several/machine-assisted) — **never** human-vs-machine — and **Amara may rebut** on the spot. Non-binding; never settles R. (Nerfed after Playtest 01, where it single-handedly canonized one answer.) |

## 6. Keeping momentum

**Read the room every ~10 minutes.** Signs of trouble and fixes:

- **A lull** → fire the next **[H]** broadcast early (M03, M09, M22, M28, M37).
- **A player standing alone** → nudge them to a named relationship: *“Have you talked to [name]? They've been asking about you.”*
- **A framework going quiet** (see the map below) → route its affirmative anchor to the floor.
- **The room “solving” it too neatly** → reintroduce affirmative evidence for a rival complete reconstruction. If they're sure it was always AI, surface Arthur's attributed letter and encounter claim; if they're sure a human wrote it, surface the no-sleep timing and misattributed human traces.

### Framework quick map (who to feed if a case is starving)
- **A / one human:** Arthur (EV21 attributed letter), Renata, Nikos.
- **B / human mantle:** Rafi, Eddie, Nadia — many faces and disputed succession.
- **C / synthetic origin:** Amara, Wren (PF18 timing), + M41/M27. Present this as possible from the beginning, not merely a takeover.
- **D / no coherent journalist:** Cody, + M28/M29 (the desk-brand root).
- **Possible crime:** Manny (dead drop), Ruiz (investigation), Eddie (footage gap). A crime around R. does not establish that its victim was R.

## 7. The endgame (2:52–3:00+)

1. **Fire M40** — disputed last words or an automated provocation; the article auto-publishes at “midnight” unless the room votes to stop it. Give ~5–10 minutes of open floor.
2. **Run the reconstruction ballot:**
   1. **Was R. ever human?** never / one human / human mantle / cannot determine.
   2. **Which complete history best fits?** A, B, C, D, or a specifically stated hybrid.
   3. **Did a crime occur around the disappearance, and what act is established?**
   4. **Was there a victim, and was that victim R.?** Vote separately.
   5. **Who bears responsibility, and for what act?**
   6. **Is the article authentic? Release it?**
3. **Announce results as the room's verdict.** *“The room has decided…”*
4. **No solution reveal:** summarize the strongest evidence and unresolved contradiction for each framework. Do not declare a true ontology.
5. **Personal scoring:** each player reports Main + Sub 1 + Sub 2. Ruiz scores the concrete case she built, including whether she kept identity, harm, and culpability distinct.
6. **Optional awards:** best-supported reconstruction, most persuasive accusation, best performance, best deception, most goals completed, best costume.

---

## 8. After the game

- **De-role (5 min):** go around — each person says their real name and one thing that is *not* true of them but was of their character.
- **Debrief / feedback:** ask which reconstruction people believed, what evidence changed their mind, whether synthetic origin and human mantle each felt affirmatively supported, and whether anyone felt sidelined. Log it in [`playtest-notes.md`](playtest-notes.md).

## 9. Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|
| The room collapses to one reading | Resurface a rival era's anchor (Arthur's letter for A, the timing for C, the desk-brand for D). The arc is contested — keep all eras fed. |
| A player is passive/lost | Host-nudge to a named relationship; hand them a reason someone needs them *now*. |
| An ability is being abused | It's fine in-fiction — but remind the table an ability never proves R.; opponents can counter socially (and Farah's verdict is rebuttable). |
| Someone takes deception personally | “Brake,” check in, off-game corner; reaffirm player-vs-character. |
| The room wants “the answer” | Explain that no ontology is canonical; ask which reconstruction best accounts for the evidence and its contradictions. |
| Running long | Compress Phase 2 timed messages; never cut Phase 3 destabilizers or M40. |

---

## 10. One-line prep summary
Assign roles → brief safety → open with M01 → walk the room feeding framework cases and firing [H] beats → escalate through M10 and M26 → fire M40 → run the reconstruction ballot → announce the room's verdict and unresolved contradictions → de-role. Never confirm a reading.
