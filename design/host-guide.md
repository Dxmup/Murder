# Host / GM Run Guide — *How Many R's in Murder?*

Everything you need to run the three-hour game for ~20 players. Read this end-to-end once before your first run. Companion data: [`data/messages.csv`](data/messages.csv) (the schedule), [`data/characters.csv`](data/characters.csv), the sheets in [`/characters`](../characters), [`data/evidence.csv`](data/evidence.csv), and [`canon.md`](canon.md).

---

## 0. The host's job (and the one rule)

You are **not** the answer. This game is **fully interpretive** — there is no hidden truth about what R. was, not even one you secretly hold ([`canon.md`](canon.md)). Your job is three things:

1. **Timing** — release the right message at the right moment so the room keeps moving.
2. **Momentum** — notice who's stuck and feed them a thread.
3. **Safety** — keep the *social* game safe even while the *fictional* game gets cutthroat.

> **The one rule you must never break:** never tell a player (or hint, or rule) that any framework is “right.” When asked “so what really happened?” your honest answer is *“That's what the room is here to decide.”*

- [ ] Assign each player a **private login code** → their character (host dashboard, or paper fallback).

- [ ] Room: one main space + a quiet **off-game corner** (see safety).
- [ ] Have the **endgame ballot** ready (§7).

## 2. Safety & tone (do not skip)

This is a game of **deception, accusation, and exposure**, and several characters carry real-world-sensitive material: undocumented status (Nikos's cook, Manny, Marisol's people), a fabricated romance, financial/political corruption, and existential AI/identity themes. Run it with a light safety scaffold:

- **Opening brief (2 min):** “Everything tonight is fiction. Your character may lie, betray, and accuse — *you* and the other player are on the same team. Play the character, respect the person.”
- **Cut / Brake:** teach two words. **“Cut”** = stop the scene now (someone's uncomfortable). **“Brake”** = keep going but dial this down. Anyone can use either, no justification needed.
- **Check-in gesture:** a discreet thumbs-tilt means “you good?” — reply thumbs up/down.
- **Off-game corner:** a marked space where anyone can step fully out of character, no questions.
- **Sensitive-theme note:** tell players up front that immigration status, surveillance, and betrayal appear in the fiction; invite anyone to flag a line they'd rather not cross and route around it.
- **De-role at the end (§8):** intense social games need a landing.

## 3. Structure at a glance

Three ~55-minute phases, each opened by a public R. broadcast, plus a ~10-min endgame.

| Phase | Question | Feel | Opens with |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** (0:00–0:55) | *Where is R.?* | Orientation, alliances, the routine | **M01** (R. to all) |
| **2** (1:00–1:58) | *Who is R.?* | Contradictions, the identity fight | **M10** (wire: “maybe not one person”) |
| **3** (2:00–2:52) | *Was there ever an R.?* | Destabilize, deal-making, panic | **M26** (R.: “never only me”) |
| **End** (2:52–3:00) | *The vote* | Decision, publish-or-not | **M40** (auto-publish threat) |

## 5. Adjudicating the eight abilities

Only eight characters have a mechanic. Resolve each at the table, out loud or via the app. **Golden rule: no ability resolves what R. was.**

| Character | Ability | How you rule it |
|---|---|---|
| Eleanor (C10) | **Publish / Hold** | She controls the article's fate. At M40 the room can *override* her via the vote — that tension is the point. |
| Wren (C09) | **Forensics** | Once/phase, give a verdict on **one message/device's** authenticity or origin. You feed her the *provenance* (spoof? scheduled? impersonated?) — never “R. was X.” She may lie about it to a buyer. |
| Ruiz (C12) | **Compel** | Once, she forces a player to hand over **one physical document/evidence prop**. It compels the object, not the truth. |
| Vivian (C06) | **Buy / Bury** | Corporate money/NDAs: purchase silence or a prop, or issue a denial (M37). Track deals; they're binding in-fiction. |
| Grayson (C05) | **Leverage** | Binding financial offers (pay for silence / a claim / a document). Money buys what someone *says*, never the truth. |
| Rick (C16) | **Print It** | Push a story to the public feed — lands as “fact” even if false. Great momentum tool; let it ripple. |
| Cody (C11) | **Go Live** | Broadcast an unverified claim to the whole room. Same as Print It, louder. |
| Farah (C18) | **Expert Verdict** | She declares “N hands / a machine.” Others treat it as authoritative; it is **non-binding** and never settles R. |

## 6. Keeping momentum

**Read the room every ~10 minutes.** Signs of trouble and fixes:

- **A knot of players stuck / a lull** → fire the next **[H]** broadcast early (M03, M09, M22, M28, M37 are your momentum valves).
- **A player standing alone** → walk over as “the host” and nudge: *“Have you talked to [a name from their relationships list]? They've been asking about you.”* Every sheet lists 4–7 targets — use them.
- **A framework going quiet** (see the champion map below) → route its next fact to the floor. If **C** is dying, push M41/M27 harder; if **D** is dying, push M28/M29; if **B** is dying, spotlight Arthur/Nadia; if **A** is dying, let the fabricators be believed a while.
- **The mystery “solved” too early** → remind the room (via a witness or a message) that no one ever *saw* R.; drop a phase-3 destabilizer.

### Framework-champion quick map (who to feed if it's starving)
- **A** (one human): Renata, Nikos, Rafi, Ruiz — belief, never proof.
- **B** (mantle): Arthur, Nadia, Farah — succession + authorship.
- **C** (rogue AI): Amara, Wren, + M12/M41/M27.
- **D** (never existed): Cody, Eddie, + M28/M29.

## 7. The endgame (2:52–3:00+)

1. **Fire M40.** The article auto-publishes at “midnight” unless the room votes to stop it. Give ~5–10 minutes of open floor — final accusations, last deals, Eleanor's call, Ruiz's move.
2. **Run the ballot.** Every player votes (app or paper). Suggested questions (§17):
   1. Was R. **one human / multiple humans / an AI persona / no coherent entity**?
   2. What happened to R. (murder / voluntary / silenced / never was)?
   3. Who bears the most responsibility?
   4. Is the article authentic?
   5. **Should the article be released?** (this one has consequences — tally live)
3. **Announce results as the room's verdict, not the truth.** *“The room has decided…”* — never *“actually, R. was…”* There is no actually.
4. **Personal scoring (private or read aloud):** each player reports Main + Sub 1 + Sub 2. Some are app/host-verifiable (did the article publish? did Renata get recognized?), some are player-declared.
5. **Optional awards:** best-supported theory, most persuasive accusation, best performance, best deception, most goals completed, best costume. Purely for fun; separate from the core.

---

## 8. After the game

- **De-role (5 min):** go around the circle — each person says their real name and one thing that is *not* true of them but was true of their character. Breaks the fiction cleanly.
- **Debrief / feedback:** ask which framework people believed and *why*, and whether anyone felt sidelined. **This is your real balance test** — the pressure-tests only approximate a live table. Note anything for the next run in a new `playtest-notes.md`.

---

## 9. Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|
| One framework is “winning” by 1:30 | Feed its opposites (champion map §6); drop a destabilizer early. |
| A player is passive/lost | Host-nudge to a named relationship; hand them a reason someone needs them *now*. |
| An ability is being abused | It's fine in-fiction — but remind the table an ability never proves R.; let opponents counter socially. |
| Someone takes deception personally | “Brake,” check in, off-game corner; reaffirm player-vs-character. |
| The room wants “the answer” at the end | Hold the line: the vote *is* the answer. That discomfort is the design. |
| Running long | Compress Phase 2 timed messages; never cut Phase 3 destabilizers or M40. |

---

## 10. One-line prep summary
Assign roles → brief safety → open with M01 → walk the room feeding threads and firing [H] beats on the clock → escalate through M10 and M26 → fire M40 → vote → announce the *room's* verdict → de-role. Never say what R. was.
