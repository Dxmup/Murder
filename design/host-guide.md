# Host / GM Run Guide — *How Many R's in Murder?*

Everything you need to run the three-hour game for ~20 players. Read this end-to-end once before your first run. Companion data: [`data/messages.csv`](data/messages.csv) (the schedule), [`data/characters.csv`](data/characters.csv), the sheets in [`/characters`](../characters), [`data/evidence.csv`](data/evidence.csv), [`canon.md`](canon.md), and — **host eyes only** — [`canon-timeline.md`](canon-timeline.md) (the true arc of R.).

---

## 0. The host's job (and the one rule)

The truth of R. is a **layered, sequential arc** — one human, then a mantle, then an AI, then a last human who tried to reclaim it and was erased ([`canon-timeline.md`](canon-timeline.md)). **You hold that arc in your pocket.** Each player is a sincere witness to just one era. Your job is three things:

1. **Timing** — release the right message at the right moment so the room keeps moving.
2. **Momentum** — notice who's stuck and feed them a thread.
3. **Safety** — keep the *social* game safe even while the *fictional* game gets cutthroat.

> **The one rule you must never break:** never confirm any reading *during* play. When asked “so what really happened?” your answer is *“That's what the room is here to piece together.”* The canonical arc — if you reveal it at all — comes **only after the final vote**, and it's optional (§7).

## 2. Safety & tone (do not skip)

This is a game of **deception, accusation, and exposure**, and several characters carry real-world-sensitive material: undocumented status (Nikos's cook, Manny, Marisol's people), a fabricated romance, financial/political corruption, and existential AI/identity themes. Run it with a light safety scaffold:

- **Opening brief (2 min):** “Everything tonight is fiction. Your character may lie, betray, and accuse — *you* and the other player are on the same team. Play the character, respect the person.”
- **Cut / Brake:** teach two words. **“Cut”** = stop the scene now. **“Brake”** = keep going but dial this down. Anyone can use either, no justification needed.
- **Check-in gesture:** a discreet thumbs-tilt means “you good?”
- **Off-game corner:** a marked space where anyone can step fully out of character.
- **Sensitive-theme note:** tell players up front that immigration status, surveillance, and betrayal appear in the fiction; invite anyone to flag a line to route around.
- **De-role at the end (§8).**

## 3. Structure at a glance

Three ~55-minute phases, each opened by a public R. broadcast, plus a ~10-min endgame. The three phases happen to map onto the arc the room is (unknowingly) excavating: the recent disappearance → the identity’s history → what R. had become and who erased the last one.

| Phase | Question | Feel | Opens with |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** (0:00–0:55) | *Where is R.?* | Orientation, alliances, the routine | **M01** (R. to all) |
| **2** (1:00–1:58) | *Who is R.?* | Contradictions, the identity fight | **M10** (wire: “maybe not one person”) |
| **3** (2:00–2:52) | *Was there ever an R.?* | Destabilize, deal-making, panic | **M26** (R.: “never only me”) |
| **End** (2:52–3:00) | *The vote* | Reconstruct the arc; publish-or-not | **M40** (reclaimer's last words) |

## 5. Adjudicating the eight abilities

Only eight characters have a mechanic. **Golden rule: no ability resolves what R. was.**

| Character | Ability | How you rule it |
|---|---|---|
| Eleanor (C10) | **Publish / Hold** | Controls the article's fate; can **freeze ONE track at a time** (one person's publish/leak/sale) — *not* the room. The M40 vote can override her. (Fixed after Playtest 01: the old version froze everyone in Phase 1.) |
| Wren (C09) | **Forensics** | Once/phase, a verdict on **one message/device's** authenticity or origin. You feed her the *provenance* — never “R. was X.” She may lie to a buyer. |
| Ruiz (C12) | **Compel** | Once, force a player to surrender **one physical document/prop**. Compels the object, not the truth. |
| Vivian (C06) | **Buy / Bury** | Meridian money/NDAs: purchase silence or a prop, or issue the denial (M37). Track deals; binding in-fiction. The model ability — it *preserves* ambiguity. |
| Grayson (C05) | **Leverage** | Binding financial offers. Money buys what someone *says*, never the truth. |
| Rick (C16) | **Print It** | Push a story to the public feed (lands as “fact” even if false). **Forcing trigger:** if he's uncovered Eleanor's 7-year gap by Phase 3 and she hasn't given him an exclusive, he may auto-print it. |
| Cody (C11) | **Go Live** | Broadcast an unverified claim to the whole room. Momentum tool; let it ripple. |
| Farah (C18) | **Expert Verdict** | She declares only **how many hands** (one/several/machine-assisted) — **never** human-vs-machine — and **Amara may rebut** on the spot. Non-binding; never settles R. (Nerfed after Playtest 01, where it single-handedly canonized one answer.) |

## 6. Keeping momentum

**Read the room every ~10 minutes.** Signs of trouble and fixes:

- **A lull** → fire the next **[H]** broadcast early (M03, M09, M22, M28, M37).
- **A player standing alone** → nudge them to a named relationship: *“Have you talked to [name]? They've been asking about you.”*
- **An era going quiet** (see the map below) → route its anchor to the floor.
- **The room “solving” it too neatly** → remember the arc is *contested*: reintroduce a fact that muddies the transition they think they've nailed (e.g., if they're sure it's “just an AI,” resurface Arthur's founder letter).

### Era-witness quick map (who to feed if a thread is starving)
- **A / one human (founder):** Arthur (EV21 the letter), Renata, Nikos.
- **B / the mantle:** Rafi, Eddie, Nadia — many faces, the succession.
- **C / the machine:** Amara, Wren (PF18 timing), + M41/M27.
- **D / never one coherent thing:** Cody, + M28/M29 (the desk-brand root).
- **The erasure (endgame spine):** Manny (the dead-drop), Ruiz (the victim), Eddie (the footage gap).

## 7. The endgame (2:52–3:00+)

1. **Fire M40** — the reclaimer's last words; the article auto-publishes at “midnight” unless the room votes to stop it. Give ~5–10 minutes of open floor.
2. **Run the arc ballot.** The old “pick one of four” let a compromise reading win; this rewards *reconstruction*:
   1. **The arc:** did R. **change over its 15 years** — one human, then many, then a machine — or was it **always one thing**? (Award recognition to players who piece the sequence together.)
   2. **What happened to the last R.?** murdered / ruined / supplanted / walked away (unprovable — the room decides what it believes).
   3. **Who bears the most responsibility?** the AI / Meridian / a mantle-holder / no one.
   4. **Is the article authentic?**
   5. **Release the article?** (tally live — it has consequences).
3. **Announce results as the room's verdict.** *“The room has decided…”*
4. **Optional reveal:** *now, and only now,* you may read the canonical arc from [`canon-timeline.md`](canon-timeline.md) — or keep it in your pocket and let the room's verdict stand. Your call, per group. (A room that reconstructed the arc earns a satisfying “you were all right, in turn”; a room that wants to keep arguing can be left to.)
5. **Personal scoring:** each player reports Main + Sub 1 + Sub 2. Ruiz now has a **real victim** to have found (the erased last human) — the case can actually close.
6. **Optional awards:** best-reconstructed arc, most persuasive accusation, best performance, best deception, most goals completed, best costume.

---

## 8. After the game

- **De-role (5 min):** go around — each person says their real name and one thing that is *not* true of them but was of their character.
- **Debrief / feedback:** ask which era people believed R. “really” was, whether they saw the sequence, and whether anyone felt sidelined. Log it in [`playtest-notes.md`](playtest-notes.md).

## 9. Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|
| The room collapses to one reading | Resurface a rival era's anchor (Arthur's letter for A, the timing for C, the desk-brand for D). The arc is contested — keep all eras fed. |
| A player is passive/lost | Host-nudge to a named relationship; hand them a reason someone needs them *now*. |
| An ability is being abused | It's fine in-fiction — but remind the table an ability never proves R.; opponents can counter socially (and Farah's verdict is rebuttable). |
| Someone takes deception personally | “Brake,” check in, off-game corner; reaffirm player-vs-character. |
| The room wants “the answer” | Hold the line until after the vote; then reveal the arc, or don't — your call. |
| Running long | Compress Phase 2 timed messages; never cut Phase 3 destabilizers or M40. |

---

## 10. One-line prep summary
Assign roles → brief safety → open with M01 → walk the room feeding era-threads and firing [H] beats → escalate through M10 and M26 → fire M40 → run the arc ballot → announce the room's verdict → optionally reveal the true arc → de-role. Never confirm a reading mid-game.
