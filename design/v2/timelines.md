# Version 2 Interlocking Timelines

## Status and notation

This is the first objective-event scaffold for V2. Dates use `T` for the anniversary gathering and relative offsets because the exact 2027 event date is not yet selected.

Each entry separates:

- **Objective event:** what the design may treat as having occurred;
- **Created traces:** records or memories that can enter play;
- **Unresolved meaning:** interpretations the event cannot settle.

No objective event states that R was human, that a complete article existed, or that Vale was murdered.

## Cross-timeline joins

- RH01/E01 and VT01–VT02 connect Vale to the human-origin question.
- RH04–RH06/E02 and VT03 connect Vale and Jules to the mantle question.
- RH09–RH11/E06–E07 and VT05–VT07 connect Vale to possible autonomous control.
- AR01–AR10 overlap Vale's investigation without establishing that Vale originated or believed the rumor.
- VT11–VT14 provide a possible incident sequence without establishing that Vale died.
- AR10 and VT15 create the event's dramatic pressure without confirming that R—or a live sender—summoned the room.

## Timeline guardrails

1. No witness sees Vale killed or sees a body.
2. No record identifies a person at the service exit as Vale or R.
3. The post-Vale intervention has a plausible nonlethal function.
4. The adaptive R interval lacks proof that every possible human operator was absent.
5. Every article reference is attributed language, a partial trace, or someone else's reaction.
6. The persistent habit has at least one observable discontinuity and at least one plausible continuity explanation.
7. The early paper letter can be dated but not authored.
8. The exact transition dates required by the progressive ending are reconstructed, never printed as labels.
