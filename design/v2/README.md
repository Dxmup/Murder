# Version 2 — Rebuild

Version 2 is a clean-sheet reconstruction of *How Many R's in Murder?* The existing roster, sheets, messages, and simulations remain useful historical material, but they are not constraints on this version.

Start with [`core-concept.md`](core-concept.md), then [`cast-and-mechanics.md`](cast-and-mechanics.md), [`ending-evidence-matrix.md`](ending-evidence-matrix.md), and [`messaging.md`](messaging.md). The cast and provisional relationship graph also exist as machine-readable tables in [`../data/characters.csv`](../data/characters.csv) and [`../data/relationships.csv`](../data/relationships.csv).

The ending/evidence matrix now has a first draft. [`timelines.md`](timelines.md) establishes the interlocking R-history, article-rumor, and Vale sequences, and [`atomic-facts.csv`](atomic-facts.csv) decomposes the evidence clusters into forty-two provisional facts. [`fact-distribution-audit.md`](fact-distribution-audit.md) measures access and centrality. [`goal-interaction-matrix.csv`](goal-interaction-matrix.csv) and its [`audit`](goal-interaction-audit.md) define playable success conditions and verify at least four incoming and outgoing interaction routes for every core role. [`objects-and-bargains.md`](objects-and-bargains.md) and [`bargain-matrix.csv`](bargain-matrix.csv) define voluntary circulation. [`simulation-messages.csv`](simulation-messages.csv) and its [`audit`](simulation-message-audit.md) provide the minimum three-section test schedule. Abbreviated-brief generation and V2 schemas live in [`../../simulation/v2/`](../../simulation/v2/). Next, implement and dry-run the deterministic V2 runner.
