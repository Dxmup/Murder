# Version 2 — Rebuild

Version 2 is a clean-sheet reconstruction of *How Many R's in Murder?* The existing roster, sheets, messages, and simulations remain useful historical material, but they are not constraints on this version.

Start with [`core-concept.md`](core-concept.md), then [`cast-and-mechanics.md`](cast-and-mechanics.md), [`ending-evidence-matrix.md`](ending-evidence-matrix.md), and [`messaging.md`](messaging.md). The cast and provisional relationship graph also exist as machine-readable tables in [`../data/characters.csv`](../data/characters.csv) and [`../data/relationships.csv`](../data/relationships.csv).

The ending/evidence matrix now has a first draft. [`timelines.md`](timelines.md) establishes the interlocking R-history, article-rumor, and Vale sequences, and [`atomic-facts.csv`](atomic-facts.csv) contains forty-four provisional facts. [`fact-distribution-audit.md`](fact-distribution-audit.md) measures access and centrality. [`goal-interaction-matrix.csv`](goal-interaction-matrix.csv) and its [`audit`](goal-interaction-audit.md) define playable success conditions and verify at least four incoming and outgoing interaction routes for every core role. [`objects-and-bargains.md`](objects-and-bargains.md) and [`bargain-matrix.csv`](bargain-matrix.csv) define voluntary circulation. [`simulation-messages.csv`](simulation-messages.csv) and its [`audit`](simulation-message-audit.md) provide the minimum three-section test schedule. The deterministic isolated-agent runner, abbreviated briefs, and V2 schemas live in [`../../simulation/v2/`](../../simulation/v2/).

The first V2 baseline is complete; see [`baseline-01-findings.md`](baseline-01-findings.md). A controlled second-run revision removes answer-shaped progressive vocabulary, requires evidence-cited ballots, and makes commitments stateful while preserving the successful article and Vale-fate structures.

[`baseline-02-findings.md`](baseline-02-findings.md) shows that the controlled revision reduced Progressive from 14 to 12 votes but did not produce a One Human or Synthetic Origin advocate. Baseline 03 preparation adds a protected human-continuity ritual and a pre-byline machine-persona manifest as rival affirmative chains without deleting Kit's deliberately complete theory.

[`baseline-03-findings.md`](baseline-03-findings.md) records the overcorrection: Human Mantle won 15–1 because rival evidence arrived too late and collaboration remained sufficient for a mantle vote. Baseline 04 will move the rival evidence earlier and require theory-specific evidence combinations.
