Removing player-facing key vocabulary and the public recitation of Vale's three statements reduced Progressive dominance but did not restore primary-ending balance. The remaining problem is therefore evidentiary as well as presentational: Progressive absorbs human collaboration and technical evidence, while One Human and Synthetic Origin lack durable affirmative late-game cases.

Baseline 02 also moved Vale's fate from unanimous insufficient evidence to a 10–6 preference for voluntary disappearance. That is not an ending unlock, but it suggests the old-safety-plan evidence may be more salient than competing positive traces.

## Design constraint

Kit must retain the complete progressive story; that is a deliberate character premise. The correction cannot simply delete Kit's theory. Instead:

- Kit's other theories and credibility costs must compete for attention;
- no game mechanic or public message should organize the room around Kit's theory;
- Lone Author and Synthetic Origin need affirmative evidence and motivated advocates;
- Progressive must cite an ordered sequence, not merely the coexistence of human, collective, and technical activity;
- Vale's disappearance needs a comparably salient trace inconsistent with voluntary escape while still avoiding proof of harm.

## Next revision

Add one non-authenticating but resilient continuity chain for One Human and one origin-specific trace for Synthetic Origin. Then define transition requirements that Progressive cannot satisfy merely by collecting both. Preserve Kit's full belief, article ambiguity, and the absence of homicide proof.
