# Character Goals (Next Step #6 draft)

Main goal + two sub-goals for all 20. Source of truth is [`data/characters.csv`](data/characters.csv); this is the readable view plus the interaction map.

## Two hard rules every goal obeys
1. **Winnable without solving the mystery (§7).** A character can complete all three goals while being wrong — or indifferent — about who/what the journalist was. (e.g. C14 just wants the restaurant kept clean of scandal; C20 wants the tab paid; C09 wants the highest bidder.)
2. **No goal, if achieved, proves a human existed.** Goals trade in *claims, belief, recognition, money, and narrative* — never in a body. Estate recognition, publication, a settled tab, a sold story: none of these confirm a single living author, so Frameworks C and D survive even when goals succeed.

## Interaction hubs (where the traffic concentrates)
- **The article → C10.** C05, C06, C07, C08, C17, C18 all need something from the editor. The busiest node.
- **Exposure → C16.** The tabloid is sought by sellers (C14, C15, C20) and feared by fabricators (C01, C02, C03, C04) and the councilwoman (C13).
- **The data → C09.** For sale to C06, C05, C04, C16; hunted by C12; needed by C08 and C18.
- **Estate & succession → C01 ↔ C03 ↔ C04.** A three-way claim fight the reporter (C16) and believer (C17) feed on.
- **The police case → C12.** A threat the working-world characters (C14, C15, C19, C20) maneuver around; C07 shields them.

## Framework safety check (spot pressure-test — Next Step #8)
- **A stays alive:** C01/C14/C20 pursue goals premised on a real person — but *recognition/scandal/tab* can all resolve without proof.
- **B stays alive:** C03/C04/C18 goals actively assert multiplicity or succession.
- **C stays alive:** C08/C06/C09 goals turn on whether it was an AI — unresolved by design.
- **D stays alive:** C11 profits from “never existed”; C15/C20 contradictions supply the ammunition.
- **No goal is a landmine:** none *requires* proving a single living author to be completed. Re-run this check after facts are assigned.
