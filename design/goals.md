# Character Goals and Interaction Design

Every core character has one main goal, two supporting goals, and a consequential final choice unrelated to guessing the mystery correctly. Goals create reciprocal movement through the party via private meetings, interruptions, offers, needs, protection, attribution, access, money, and public commitments.

The normalized source is [`data/goals.csv`](data/goals.csv). Bargaining resources and consequences are in [`data/bargains.csv`](data/bargains.csv); the readable player-facing versions are in [`../characters/`](../characters/). [`goal-interaction-audit.md`](goal-interaction-audit.md) checks incoming and outgoing interaction routes.

Design rules:

- No goal requires compelled truth, testimony, cooperation, or object surrender.
- A mystery vote is not a success condition.
- Each player must need several other people and be needed in return.
- Personal loyalty, reputation, livelihood, privacy, and ideology remain meaningful under every ending.
- Commitments can be kept, renegotiated, or betrayed with social consequences.
